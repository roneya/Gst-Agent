# DRC-01A — Pre-SCN Intimation (Opportunity Before Show Cause Notice)

## What Is a DRC-01A?

DRC-01A is an **intimation issued by the proper officer before issuing a formal Show Cause Notice (DRC-01)**. It gives the taxpayer an opportunity to pay the alleged short-paid tax, interest, and penalty voluntarily before the SCN stage.

- Governed by: Section 73(5) / 74(5) / 74A of CGST Act, 2017
- Rule 142(1A) of CGST Rules, 2017 prescribes DRC-01A format

---

## Is DRC-01A Mandatory?

| Section | DRC-01A Required? |
|---|---|
| Section 73 (non-fraud) | Optional for officer — taxpayer can respond or ignore |
| Section 74 (fraud) | Not typically issued (officer may directly issue DRC-01) |
| Section 74A (FY 2024-25+) | As prescribed under new provisions |

> **Key point:** DRC-01A is a pre-notice opportunity, not a mandatory condition precedent to DRC-01 for most cases. However, if DRC-01A is issued and the taxpayer pays in full, the officer cannot issue a DRC-01 SCN for that demand.

---

## Timeline and Options on Receipt

```
DRC-01A received
    ↓
Taxpayer has 30 days to respond
    ↓
Option A: Pay in full (tax + interest + 25% penalty) → No DRC-01 SCN
Option B: Pay in part → DRC-01 SCN issued for balance
Option C: Dispute → No payment → Officer proceeds to issue DRC-01
```

---

## Immediate Checklist on Receipt of DRC-01A

1. **Verify the demand amount** — check the computation attached to DRC-01A
2. **Calculate 30-day response window** — flag if < 7 days
3. **Check limitation** — confirm the underlying demand is within the applicable period under §73/§74/§74A
4. **Assess merit** — is the demand correct, partially correct, or completely wrong?
5. **Compute cost of paying now vs contesting** — compare penalty at DRC-01A stage vs SCN stage

---

## Penalty Comparison — Why Paying at DRC-01A Stage Saves Money

| Stage | Penalty (Section 73 — Non-Fraud) |
|---|---|
| Before DRC-01A / DRC-01 (voluntary DRC-03) | **Nil** |
| Within 30 days of DRC-01A | **10% of tax** (min ₹10,000) |
| After DRC-01 SCN — within 30 days | **10% of tax** (min ₹10,000) |
| After DRC-07 adjudication order | **Up to 10% of tax** (non-fraud) |

## How to Respond

### If Accepting the Demand in Full

1. Pay the tax + interest under §50 via DRC-03 on GST portal
2. Penalty @ 25% of tax (for §73 cases) — pay via DRC-03
3. After payment, file DRC-01A response on portal referencing DRC-03 ARN
4. The officer should then **not** issue a DRC-01 SCN for amounts paid

**GST Portal Path:**
```
Services → User Services → My Applications
→ Select DRC-01A reference → File Response → Pay via DRC-03
```

### If Partially Accepting

- Pay the undisputed portion via DRC-03
- Clearly state in the DRC-01A response which portion is disputed and why
- Be prepared — officer will issue DRC-01 SCN for the disputed balance

### If Disputing Entirely

- File a written response within 30 days contesting the demand
- State grounds of dispute clearly (limitation, factual errors, legal grounds)
- No payment required at this stage
- Officer will then decide whether to issue formal DRC-01 SCN

---

## Strategic Considerations

**Pay at DRC-01A stage when:**
- The demand is clearly correct (saves further litigation cost)
- The amount is small and the legal position is weak
- The taxpayer wants to avoid formal SCN / adjudication on record

**Contest at DRC-01A stage when:**
- The demand is factually wrong (supplier has filed GSTR-1, ITC is valid)
- The notice is time-barred
- The legal position is strong (supported by HC / SC / Circular)
- The amount is significant enough to justify litigation

---

## Key Legal Points

- Payment via DRC-03 at DRC-01A stage attracts **reduced / nil penalty** under §73(5)
- Once DRC-01 SCN is issued, the taxpayer loses the §73(5) reduced-penalty window
- Interest under §50 runs from the original due date, not the DRC-01A date
