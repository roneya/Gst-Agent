# ADT-01 — GST Audit Notice (Section 65)

## What Is ADT-01?

ADT-01 is the **notice issued to a registered person informing them of an upcoming GST audit** under Section 65 of the CGST Act, 2017. It initiates a formal examination of the taxpayer's books, records, and returns by the proper officer.

Load `section-65.md` for full statutory provisions.

---

## ADT-01 vs ASMT-10 — Know the Difference

| | ASMT-10 (Scrutiny) | ADT-01 (Audit) |
|---|---|---|
| Scope | Specific return discrepancy | Full books examination |
| Risk level | Medium | High |
| Officer visits premises? | Usually No | **Yes** |
| Duration | Short | Up to 3 months |
| Findings → | ASMT-12 or SCN | ADT-02 report then SCN |

## Audit Lifecycle

```
ADT-01 Notice (15 working days minimum advance)
        ↓
Audit commences — officer examines books, records, invoices
        ↓
Officer raises queries / calls for additional documents
        ↓
Draft findings communicated to taxpayer (opportunity to reply)
        ↓
Taxpayer submits written rebuttal to draft findings
        ↓
ADT-02 — Audit Report issued
        ↓
If discrepancy → DRC-01 SCN under §73/74/74A
        ↓
Taxpayer files DRC-06 reply
        ↓
DRC-07 Order
```

## Immediate Checklist on Receiving ADT-01

1. **Check 15-working-day notice period** — If less than 15 working days given → write to officer requesting postponement; it's a legal right
2. **Note audit start date and duration** — Block the period in your calendar
3. **Identify scope of audit** — Which FYs? Specific allegations or general?
4. **Appoint CA / CMA / advocate immediately** — Professional must accompany or represent
5. **File all pending returns** — Before audit starts, file any overdue GSTR-1, GSTR-3B, GSTR-9
6. **Organise all records** (see below)
7. **Conduct internal pre-audit review** — Find issues before the officer does; pay DRC-03 voluntarily to save penalty

## Document Preparation Checklist

Organise period-wise (FY by FY):

**Statutory Records:**
- [ ] Books of accounts (Tally / ERP data + printed ledgers)
- [ ] Purchase register (invoice-wise with GSTIN of suppliers)
- [ ] Sales register (invoice-wise with customer GSTIN / details)
- [ ] ITC register (monthly ITC availed, reversed, closing balance)
- [ ] GSTR-1 filed (all months / quarters)
- [ ] GSTR-3B filed (all months)
- [ ] GSTR-9 and GSTR-9C filed
- [ ] GSTR-2A / GSTR-2B downloaded for each month

**Financial Records:**
- [ ] Bank statements (all accounts)
- [ ] Cash book
- [ ] Journal entries for GST transactions
- [ ] Audited financial statements / balance sheet

**Transaction Records:**
- [ ] Purchase invoices (original)
- [ ] Sale invoices (copies)
- [ ] E-way bills (generated / received)
- [ ] Delivery challans / lorry receipts
- [ ] Import / export documents (if applicable)
- [ ] Contracts / service agreements

**Payment Records:**
- [ ] GST payment challans
- [ ] DRC-03 payments (if any)
- [ ] Bank transfer proofs to suppliers (for ITC verification)

## How to Handle the Audit Officer

### Do:
- Be cooperative and polite — adversarial attitude worsens outcomes
- Have a CA / advocate present at all times
- Provide only what is specifically asked for
- Give written responses to written queries (not verbal)
- Request time if additional documents are needed — don't give incomplete docs
- Keep copies of everything submitted to the officer

### Don't:
- Don't volunteer information beyond what is asked
- Don't make oral admissions — always respond in writing
- Don't sign any document without CA review
- Don't allow officer to take original records — certified copies only
- Don't panic — the audit is a process, not a conviction

## When Officer Communicates Draft Findings

This is the **most critical stage** — before ADT-02 is finalised:

1. Get the draft findings in writing (insist on this)
2. Analyse each finding with your CA
3. **Pay correct amounts via DRC-03 immediately** for accepted findings — no penalty at this stage
4. **File detailed written rebuttal** for disputed findings within the time given
5. Request a personal discussion with the audit officer if needed

**For each finding in rebuttal:**
```
Finding: [Copy officer's finding]
Our Position: [Factual + legal response]
Documents: [List annexures enclosed]
Prayer: Drop this finding / Reduce demand to ₹X
```

---

## Responding to ADT-02 (Audit Report)

Once ADT-02 is formally issued:
- If demand is raised → DRC-01 SCN will follow
- Load `drc-01-scn.md` and `drc-06-reply.md`
- The DRC-06 reply must specifically address each ADT-02 finding
- Refer to your written rebuttal to draft findings as evidence that your position was already on record

---

## Voluntary Payment During Audit (Strategic)

If the internal pre-audit review reveals genuine defaults:
- Pay via **DRC-03** before the audit commences / before SCN is issued
- Under §73(5) → **Nil penalty** for voluntary payment before SCN
- Mention the DRC-03 payment in writing to the officer immediately
- This prevents the officer from alleging suppression for amounts already paid
