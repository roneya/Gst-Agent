## PART C — PROCEDURAL GROUNDS

*(Raise applicable objections against the DRC-07 order itself)*

**Procedural Ground 1 — Non-Speaking Order** *(if applicable)*
> The impugned order merely reproduces the demand figures from the SCN without recording any reasons for rejecting the Appellant's DRC-06 reply. An order without reasons is not a "speaking order" as mandated by Section 75(5) of the CGST Act, 2017. The impugned order is liable to be set aside on this ground alone and the matter remanded for fresh adjudication after considering the Appellant's reply.

**Procedural Ground 2 — Personal Hearing Not Granted** *(if applicable)*
> The Appellant specifically requested a personal hearing in the DRC-06 reply under Section 75(3) of the CGST Act, 2017. No personal hearing was granted. Passing an order without affording the mandatory personal hearing is a violation of the principles of natural justice and Section 75(3). The order is void on this ground.

**Procedural Ground 3 — Demand Beyond SCN** *(if applicable)*
> The impugned DRC-07 order confirms a demand of ₹[X], which exceeds the demand of ₹[Y] as stated in the SCN. Section 107(11) of the CGST Act, 2017 expressly prohibits enhancement of demand beyond the SCN amount. The demand to the extent of ₹[X−Y] is beyond jurisdiction and is liable to be deleted.

**Procedural Ground 4 — Time-Barred Order** *(if applicable)*
> The DRC-07 order dated [date] is passed beyond the period prescribed under Section 75(7) of the CGST Act, 2017. For FY [XX], the maximum period for passing the order was [calculated date]. The impugned order is barred by limitation and is void.

## PART D — LEGAL SUBMISSIONS

> 1. **ITC eligibility under §16(2):** The Appellant has fulfilled all conditions under Section 16(2) — tax invoice received, goods/services received, tax paid to supplier, and GSTR-3B filed. ITC cannot be denied when substantive conditions are met. [Cite case law]
>
> 2. **GSTR-2B is not conclusive:** Rule 36(4) restricts current-period availment but does not permanently deny eligible ITC. [Cite: Dyna Automation, Suncraft Energy]
>
> 3. **Penalty — Section 73 cap:** Where no fraud is established, penalty cannot exceed 10% of tax (§73). The officer's application of §74 penalty at 25%/100% is unsustainable without evidence of fraud, willful misstatement, or suppression. [Cite case law]
>
> 4. **Interest on net cash liability:** As per Finance Act 2021 amendment to §50(1), interest applies only on the cash portion of tax liability, not on ITC set-off. The officer's interest calculation on gross liability is incorrect.
>
> 5. **Natural justice:** Audi alteram partem — both sides must be heard before an adverse order is passed. [Cite: Asstt. Commissioner v. Shukla & Brothers (SC)]
