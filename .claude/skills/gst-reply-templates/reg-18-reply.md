## REG-18 Reply Template

---

**To,**
The Proper Officer
[Designation]
[GST Office Address]

**Subject:** Reply to REG-17 Show Cause Notice — GSTIN: [GSTIN] — Trade Name: [Name]

**REG-18 — Reply to Show Cause Notice for Cancellation of Registration**

Date: [Date]

---

### PART A — REFERENCE

We refer to your Show Cause Notice in REG-17 dated [Date], bearing DIN [DIN Number] / reference no. [Reference], proposing to cancel GST registration of:

- **Trade Name:** [Name]
- **GSTIN:** [GSTIN]
- **Registered Address:** [Address]
- **Effective date of proposed cancellation:** [Date if mentioned]

The notice proposes cancellation on the ground that: *[Copy ground from REG-17 — e.g., "The registered person has not filed returns for a continuous period of six months."]*

We hereby submit our reply to the above notice:

---

### PART B — GROUND-WISE REPLY

---

**Ground: Non-Filing of Returns** *(Most common — use if applicable)*

> We sincerely regret the delay in filing returns. The delay occurred due to [reason — e.g., technical issues / financial difficulty / illness of proprietor / bookkeeper error / Covid-related disruption].
>
> We are pleased to inform that all pending returns have now been filed and all outstanding dues including tax, interest, and late fee have been paid. Details are as under:
>
> | Period | Return | ARN | Date of Filing | Late Fee Paid |
> |---|---|---|---|---|
> | [Month/Quarter] | GSTR-3B | [ARN] | [Date] | ₹[Amount] |
> | [Month/Quarter] | GSTR-1 | [ARN] | [Date] | ₹[Amount] |
>
> Since the default has been fully cured, the grounds for cancellation no longer subsist. We respectfully pray that the proposed cancellation be dropped.
>
> **Annexures:**
> - Annexure 1 — GSTR-3B ARNs for all filed periods
> - Annexure 2 — GSTR-1 ARNs for all filed periods
> - Annexure 3 — Challan receipts for tax + late fee paid

---

**Ground: Business Discontinued** *(Use if allegation is business not being carried on)*

> The allegation that the business has been discontinued is factually incorrect. The taxpayer is actively carrying on business as evidenced by:
>
> - Recent purchase invoices (Annexure [X]) showing active procurement
> - Recent sale invoices (Annexure [X]) showing outward supply
> - Bank statement (Annexure [X]) showing business transactions
> - E-way bills generated (Annexure [X]) confirming movement of goods
>
> The business is a going concern and there is no intention to discontinue. The proposed cancellation is not warranted.

---

**Ground: Obtained Registration by Fraud / Misrepresentation** *(Use if fraud is alleged)*

> The allegation of fraud / misrepresentation is denied in the strongest terms. All documents submitted at the time of registration were genuine and correct. In particular:
>
> - The principal place of business at [address] is the actual place of business of the taxpayer, as evidenced by [documents enclosed]
> - The applicant's identity documents are genuine (PAN [Number] issued by Income Tax Department)
> - Business activity has been carried on since [date] as evidenced by [invoices / bank statements]
>
> There is no element of fraud, misrepresentation, or suppression of material facts. The registration was validly obtained and must be sustained.
>
> **Annexures:** [List all supporting documents]

---

### PART C — LEGAL SUBMISSIONS

> 1. The power to cancel registration under Section 29(2) of the CGST Act, 2017 is discretionary and must be exercised after considering the reply of the taxpayer.
>
> 2. Where the default (non-filing) has been cured before the passing of the cancellation order, cancellation is not warranted — the object of the notice has been achieved. [Cite: Multiple HC decisions holding that curing of default before order disentitles cancellation]
>
> 3. Cancellation of GSTIN has severe consequences on the taxpayer's business — customers cannot claim ITC on purchases, affecting business relationships. The drastic step of cancellation must be resorted to only in extreme cases.
>
> 4. The taxpayer requests a **personal hearing** under Section 75(3) before any order is passed.

---

### PART D — PRAYER

We most respectfully pray that:

1. The Show Cause Notice in REG-17 dated [Date] be dropped and the GSTIN [GSTIN] be restored / continued without cancellation;
2. A personal hearing be granted before passing any order;
3. Any other relief as deemed fit and proper.

---

## Portal Filing Path

```
Services → Registration → Application for Filing Clarifications
→ Select REG-17 reference number
→ File REG-18
→ Upload all supporting documents
→ Submit
```

**Deadline:** Within **7 days** of REG-17 notice date.

---

## If REG-19 Cancellation Order Is Still Passed

Apply for revocation via **REG-21** within 90 days:
```
Services → Registration → Application for Revocation of Cancelled Registration
→ File all pending returns first
→ Pay all outstanding dues
→ File REG-21 with proof
```
