### PART A — PRELIMINARY OBJECTIONS

*(Raise applicable objections without prejudice to merits)*

**Objection 1 — Missing / Invalid DIN** *(if applicable)*
> The impugned SCN does not bear a valid Document Identification Number (DIN) as mandated by CBIC Circular No. 128/47/2019-GST dated 23.10.2019. In the absence of a valid DIN, the notice is void ab initio and liable to be quashed. Without prejudice to the above, on merits, we submit as follows.

**Objection 2 — Barred by Limitation** *(if applicable)*
> The SCN is issued beyond the period prescribed under Section [73/74/74A] of the CGST Act, 2017. The annual return for FY [XX] was due on [date]; the extended limitation expires on [calculated date]. The SCN dated [date] is time-barred and not maintainable in law.

**Objection 3 — DRC-01A Not Served** *(if applicable)*
> No intimation in DRC-01A was served prior to issuance of this SCN, as required. The SCN is premature and procedurally defective.

**Objection 4 — Jurisdiction** *(if applicable)*
> The taxpayer's GSTIN falls under the jurisdiction of [State/Central GST Authority]. The present SCN issued by [other authority] is without jurisdiction and not maintainable.

**Objection 5 — Demand Vague / Unquantified** *(if applicable)*
> The SCN does not specify the basis of computation of the alleged demand at [ground X]. Issuance of an SCN without quantifying the demand on each ground violates the principles of natural justice and renders the notice unenforceable on that head.

### PART D — LEGAL SUBMISSIONS

> 1. **Interest computation:** Interest, if any, is payable under Section 50 of the CGST Act only on the net tax liability (i.e., cash portion), not on ITC ledger balance, as settled by the Finance Act, 2021 amendment and affirmed in [case law].
>
> 2. **Penalty — Section 73 (non-fraud):** Where allegations are not of fraud, penalty is 10% of tax or ₹10,000, whichever is higher, at the SCN stage. The demand for 100% penalty is not sustainable for non-fraud grounds.
>
> 3. **Penalty — Section 74 (fraud):** If fraud is alleged, the burden of proof lies on the Revenue to establish willful misstatement / suppression of facts with positive evidence. Mere difference in returns does not establish fraud. [Cite relevant HC/SC judgment].
>
> 4. **Natural justice:** The taxpayer reserves the right to a personal hearing under §75(4) before any order is passed.

### PRE-FILING CHECKLIST

- [ ] All SCN grounds addressed (none skipped)
- [ ] DRC-03 ARN quoted for every accepted ground
- [ ] Interest calculated correctly under §50 (load `section-50.md`)
- [ ] Annexures numbered and referenced in body
- [ ] Preliminary objections filed without prejudice to merits
- [ ] Personal hearing explicitly requested
- [ ] Filed via GST portal before deadline (30 days from SCN date)
- [ ] Extension request filed on portal if more time needed
- [ ] Hard copy / email copy sent to jurisdictional office if required
