# Section 74 — Determination of Tax (Fraud / Willful Misstatement / Suppression, up to FY 2023-24)

## ⚠️ Applicability Warning — Read First

**Section 74 applies ONLY to periods up to FY 2023-24.**

From FY 2024-25 onwards, Section 74 has been replaced by **Section 74A** (inserted by Finance Act 2024). Load `section-74a.md` for those periods.

Also critical: §74 applies **only when fraud / willful misstatement / suppression of facts is alleged**. If the SCN does not allege any of these — or if the allegations are vague and unsupported — challenge the invocation of §74 and argue that §73 applies (3-year limit, 10% penalty). This is often the most powerful ground in a §74 reply.

---

## Plain-English Summary

Section 74 is the **fraud track** demand provision. It mirrors §73 in structure but is far more serious:

| Feature | §73 (Non-fraud) | §74 (Fraud) |
|---|---|---|
| Allegation required | None (any reason) | Fraud / willful misstatement / suppression |
| Limitation | **3 years** | **5 years** |
| Penalty (after order) | **10%** of tax | **100%** of tax |
| Pre-SCN payment penalty | NIL | **15%** of tax |
| Post-SCN payment (30 days) | NIL | **25%** of tax |
| Post-order payment (30 days) | N/A | **50%** of tax |

The extra 2 years of limitation and the jump from 10% to 100% penalty make §74 an extremely high-stakes proceeding. The single most important task in any §74 reply is to **knock out the fraud/suppression allegation** — if that falls, the entire §74 proceeding falls with it, and the officer can only proceed under §73 (if within 3 years).

---

## When This Section Applies

Load this skill when the SCN / notice:
- Cites **Section 74** explicitly
- Uses words like: **"fraud", "willful misstatement", "suppression of facts", "intent to evade tax", "deliberately concealed", "knowingly"**
- Demands **penalty equivalent to tax (100%)**
- Covers periods **up to FY 2023-24**
- The form is **DRC-01** (SCN) or **DRC-07** (order)

If the notice cites §73 instead → load `section-73.md`.
If the period is FY 2024-25 onwards → load `section-74a.md`.

## Sub-Section Breakdown

### §74(1) — The Show Cause Notice (SCN)
The proper officer **shall serve notice** requiring the person to show cause why they should not pay:
- Tax shortfall
- Interest under §50
- **Penalty equivalent to the tax** (i.e., 100% penalty)

**Key words for the agent**:
- "By reason of fraud, or any wilful-misstatement or suppression of facts **to evade tax**" — all three elements (the act + the intent to evade) must be present and must be specifically alleged and proved. A bald assertion is not enough.
- The SCN must **clearly spell out** what the fraud/suppression consists of — not just use the word as a label.

---

### §74(2) — Minimum Gap Between SCN and Order
The SCN must be issued **at least 6 months before** the §74(10) order deadline.

Compare with §73(2) which requires only 3 months. The longer gap reflects the complexity of fraud investigations.

**Agent alert**: If SCN was issued less than 6 months before the §74(10) deadline, it is procedurally defective. Raise as preliminary objection.

---

### §74(3) & §74(4) — Statement for Additional Periods
Same mechanism as §73(3)/(4) — officer can serve a **statement** for additional periods instead of fresh SCN, subject to same grounds.

**Critical twist in §74(4)**: The statement for additional periods is deemed a notice **under §73(1)** — not §74(1) — for those periods where the **only ground** relied upon in the statement (excluding the fraud ground) is the same as in the original §74(1) SCN.

**What this means in practice**: If the officer extends the demand to additional periods via a §74(3) statement, those additional periods may actually be governed by **§73** (3-year limit, 10% penalty) rather than §74 — unless a fresh §74 SCN is issued for them. Check the period coverage carefully.

---

### §74(5) — Pre-SCN Voluntary Payment (with 15% penalty)
**Before** the SCN is served, the taxpayer may pay:
- Tax + interest (§50) + **15% penalty**

And inform the officer in writing.

Result: **No SCN served** for the amount paid.

Unlike §73(5) where pre-SCN payment attracts **zero penalty**, §74(5) requires **15% penalty** even for voluntary pre-SCN payment. This reflects the fraud allegation — the department still extracts a penalty even for early settlement.

**Strategy**: If the client wants to settle early in a §74 matter, pre-SCN payment at 15% is still far better than the post-order penalty of 100%.

---

### §74(6) — Officer's Duty After Pre-SCN Payment
Same as §73(6) — officer **shall not** serve SCN for the amount paid. Mandatory prohibition.

---

### §74(7) — Shortfall After Pre-SCN Payment
Same as §73(7) — officer may issue SCN only for the shortfall.

---

### §74(8) — Payment Within 30 Days of SCN = 25% Penalty
If the taxpayer pays **tax + interest + 25% penalty within 30 days of SCN**:
- All proceedings are **deemed concluded**

Unlike §73(8) where 30-day payment = zero penalty, §74(8) requires **25% penalty** for the same benefit. Still far better than the 100% penalty in the order.

**Strategy table**:
| When to pay | Penalty |
|---|---|
| Before SCN (§74(5)) | 15% |
| Within 30 days of SCN (§74(8)) | 25% |
| Within 30 days of order (§74(11)) | 50% |
| After 30 days of order | 100% |

---

### §74(9) — The Demand Order
After considering the reply, the officer determines tax + interest + **penalty equal to 100% of tax** and issues order in **DRC-07**.

---

### §74(10) — Limitation Period (5 Years) ⭐ Critical
The demand order must be issued within:
- **5 years** from the due date for filing **GSTR-9** for the relevant financial year, OR
- **5 years** from the date of **erroneous refund**

| Financial Year | GSTR-9 Due Date | §74(10) Order Deadline |
|---|---|---|
| FY 2017-18 | 31-Dec-2019 (extended) | 31-Dec-2024 |
| FY 2018-19 | 31-Aug-2020 (extended) | 31-Aug-2025 |
| FY 2019-20 | 28-Feb-2021 (extended) | 28-Feb-2026 |
| FY 2020-21 | 28-Feb-2022 | 28-Feb-2027 |
| FY 2021-22 | 31-Dec-2022 | 31-Dec-2027 |
| FY 2022-23 | 31-Dec-2023 | 31-Dec-2028 |
| FY 2023-24 | 31-Dec-2024 | 31-Dec-2029 |

---

### §74(11) — Post-Order Payment at 50% Penalty
If the taxpayer pays **tax + interest + 50% penalty within 30 days of the order**:
- All proceedings are **deemed concluded**

This is the last settlement window before the matter moves to recovery / appeal. At 50%, it is still cheaper than contesting and losing (100% penalty + costs).

---

### §74(12) — Applicability Clause
Confirms: This section applies **only to periods up to FY 2023-24**.

---

### Explanation 1 — "All proceedings" does not include §132
Settlement under §74(8) or §74(11) closes the civil tax/penalty proceedings — but does **not** bar criminal prosecution under **Section 132** (offences and penalties — imprisonment). The officer can still refer the matter for prosecution separately.

**Agent alert**: Always mention this to the client. Paying under §74(8) does not protect against a §132 prosecution if the fraud allegation is serious.

### Explanation 1(ii) — Co-noticees
Where the main person's proceedings are concluded (under §73 or §74), the proceedings against **co-noticees** (directors, partners, other persons liable under §122/§125) are also **deemed concluded** — except for §132 prosecution.

### Explanation 2 — Statutory definition of "suppression"
> "Suppression" means **non-declaration of facts or information** which a taxable person is required to declare in returns / statements / reports, **OR failure to furnish information on being asked for in writing** by the proper officer.

**This is extremely important for defence**:
- Suppression requires **deliberate non-disclosure** — an inadvertent omission, a bona fide dispute, or a difference of legal interpretation is NOT suppression.
- The burden is on the department to prove that the taxpayer **knew** the information was to be declared and **deliberately** withheld it.
- Mere non-payment or under-payment of tax, without more, is not suppression.

## The Core Defence: Knock Out the Fraud/Suppression Allegation

This is the most important section of the reply in every §74 matter. If the fraud/suppression allegation is defeated, the entire §74 proceeding collapses.

### How to challenge the fraud/suppression allegation

**Ground 1 — No positive act of fraud alleged**
The SCN must allege a **specific positive act** — what exactly did the taxpayer do that constitutes fraud or suppression? A mere assertion ("the taxpayer has suppressed turnover") without specifying what was suppressed, when, and how, is not sufficient. Cite: *Pushpam Pharmaceuticals Co. v. CCE (SC)*.

**Ground 2 — Bona fide dispute / difference of legal opinion**
If the demand arises from a difference in legal interpretation (e.g., whether a particular supply is taxable, whether a particular ITC is eligible), that is a bona fide dispute — not suppression. Suppression requires intent, which is absent in genuine disputes.

**Ground 3 — All information was available in the returns**
If the taxpayer declared all transactions in GSTR-1 and GSTR-3B, there is no concealment. Suppression cannot be alleged when the department had access to all the information in the taxpayer's own filings.

**Ground 4 — §73 is the correct provision**
If the alleged discrepancy is a mere mismatch (GSTR-1 vs GSTR-3B, GSTR-2A vs GSTR-3B, etc.) without any active concealment, §73 is the appropriate provision — not §74. The officer has wrongly invoked the extended limitation and higher penalty.

**Ground 5 — No intent to evade tax**
§74(1) requires fraud / misstatement / suppression "**to evade tax**" — the intent element must be proved separately. The officer cannot presume intent from the fact of non-payment alone.

## Procedural Defects Checklist

| # | Defect | Ground |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | SCN issued after §74(10) 5-year limitation expired | Time-barred — void |
| 2 | SCN issued less than 6 months before §74(10) deadline | Violates §74(2) |
| 3 | SCN does not specify what fraud / suppression was committed | Vague SCN — violates natural justice |
| 4 | Officer invoked §74 but the facts only support §73 | Wrong provision — §74 invocation is invalid; §73 limitation applies |
| 5 | SCN uses §74 but the limitation has run for §74; §73 limitation also expired | Entire demand is time-barred |
| 6 | §74(3) statement introduces new fraud grounds not in original §74(1) SCN | Invalid for those periods |
| 7 | SCN issued without DIN (Document Identification Number) | Invalid notice per CBIC Circular 128/47/2019 |
| 8 | Same allegation pending under §65 audit simultaneously | Parallel proceedings — stay one |

## Settlement Strategy — Which Window to Use

| Client situation | Recommended action |
|---|---|
| Fraud allegation is weak; demand has some merit | Fight on §74 invocation (knock down to §73); if §73 applies, pay within 30 days of revised SCN — zero penalty |
| Demand is fully valid, client wants to close fast | Pay before SCN under §74(5) — 15% penalty |
| SCN already served, demand is valid | Pay within 30 days of SCN under §74(8) — 25% penalty |
| Order passed, client lost at adjudication | Pay within 30 days of order under §74(11) — 50% penalty; then appeal |
| Strong defence on merits | File detailed reply; contest up to Appellate Authority / GSTAT / HC |

## Key Case Law

### On what constitutes "suppression"
**Pushpam Pharmaceuticals Co. v. Collector of Central Excise (SC, 1995)**
> Held: Suppression of facts must involve a **positive act** on the part of the assessee — merely not paying tax or filing a return with an error does not amount to suppression. There must be deliberate withholding of information with intent to evade duty/tax. This principle is consistently applied under GST.

**Commissioner of Customs v. M/s Virgo Industries (SC)**
> Held: A bona fide dispute about taxability of goods does not amount to suppression or wilful misstatement. The extended limitation cannot be invoked merely because the assessee took a view on taxability that the department disagrees with.

---

### On wrong invocation of §74 (should have been §73)
**M/s Anand Traders v. State of Gujarat (Gujarat HC, 2022)**
> Held: Where the department had access to all the relevant information through the taxpayer's own GST returns and filings, there can be no suppression. The proper officer cannot invoke §74 on the basis of a GSTR-2A mismatch alone, as all data was available to the department.

**Filatex India Ltd. v. Commissioner (Delhi HC)**
> Held: The invocation of the extended period of limitation requires the department to specifically plead and prove the elements of fraud/suppression. The burden of proof lies on the department, and it cannot be discharged by a bare allegation.

---

### On penalty — 100% is not automatic
**M/s Hindustan Steel Ltd. v. State of Orissa (SC)**
> Held (in the context of penalty generally): Penalty should not be imposed merely because it is lawful to do so. If the assessee's conduct shows a bona fide belief or a genuine dispute, penalty (especially at maximum rate) is not warranted.

---

### On §132 — criminal proceedings survive settlement
**CBIC Circular No. 31/05/2018-GST**
> Clarified: Payment of tax and penalty under §73/§74 and deemed conclusion of proceedings does not bar the department from initiating criminal prosecution under §132 for the same acts, as Explanation 1 expressly carves out §132.

## DRC-03 — How to Pay Under Section 74

### Pre-SCN payment (§74(5)) — 15% penalty
1. Portal → Services → Payments → DRC-03
2. Select cause: **"Voluntary"**
3. Pay: tax + interest (18% p.a.) + **15% of tax as penalty**
4. Write to the officer with ARN citing §74(5)
5. Officer cannot serve SCN for amount paid

### Post-SCN payment within 30 days (§74(8)) — 25% penalty
1. Portal → DRC-03 → cause: **"SCN"** → select SCN reference
2. Pay: tax + interest + **25% of tax as penalty**
3. File reply stating payment made; cite §74(8); pray for deemed conclusion

### Post-order payment within 30 days (§74(11)) — 50% penalty
1. Portal → DRC-03 → cause: **"Order"** → select DRC-07 order reference
2. Pay: tax + interest + **50% of tax as penalty**
3. Inform officer; cite §74(11); deemed conclusion

## §73 vs §74 — Quick Comparison (for reply drafting)

| Point | §73 | §74 |
|---|---|---|
| Allegation | No fraud required | Fraud / willful misstatement / suppression + intent to evade |
| Limitation | 3 years | 5 years |
| SCN gap from order | 3 months | 6 months |
| Pre-SCN penalty | NIL | 15% |
| 30-day SCN penalty | NIL | 25% |
| Order penalty | 10% or ₹10,000 (higher) | 100% |
| Post-order 30-day penalty | N/A | 50% |
| Burden of proof | Department | Department (higher — must prove fraud) |
| Criminal prosecution | Not barred | Not barred (Explanation 1) |
