# Section 75 — General Provisions Relating to Determination of Tax

## Why This Section Matters

Section 75 applies to **every demand proceeding** under §73, §74, and §74A. It sets the procedural safeguards that protect the taxpayer — personal hearing, burden of proof, penalty cap, and time limits for passing orders. Violations of §75 are strong grounds for quashing a DRC-07 order.

---

## Key Provisions

### §75(1) — Reliance on Information from Third Party
If the proper officer relies on information received from any other officer, he must give the person a reasonable opportunity of being heard **before** using that information against the taxpayer.

**Use in reply:** If the SCN cites data from DGGI / another GST officer without giving prior opportunity → procedural defect, raise objection.

---

### §75(2) — Opportunity Before Adverse Order
The proper officer shall, before passing any order under §73 or §74, give the person a reasonable opportunity of being heard.

**Use in reply:** If DRC-07 order is passed without granting a personal hearing → order is liable to be set aside on appeal under §107.

---

### §75(3) — Written Request for Personal Hearing
Where the taxpayer **requests** a personal hearing in writing (in DRC-06 reply), the proper officer **must** grant it.

**Use in reply:** Always include in DRC-06: *"The taxpayer hereby requests a personal hearing under §75(3) before any order is passed."*

---

### §75(4) — Adjournment of Personal Hearing
The proper officer may, if sufficient cause is shown, adjourn a personal hearing. Maximum **3 adjournments** permitted.

---

### §75(5) — Speaking Order
Every order passed under §73/74/74A must be a **speaking order** — reasons must be recorded in writing. An order without reasons is void.

**Use in appeal:** If DRC-07 order merely confirms the demand without addressing the taxpayer's reply → non-speaking order, ground for appeal under §107.

---

### §75(6) — Penalty Cap for Non-Fraud
Where the proper officer determines that **the amount of tax correctly payable is nil**, no penalty shall be imposed.

Where the proper officer issues an order under §73 and the amount of tax determined is less than the amount stated in the SCN, the penalty shall be calculated **on the tax actually determined, not on the SCN amount**.

**Use in reply:** If SCN demands ₹10L but only ₹2L is ultimately confirmed → penalty is 10% of ₹2L, not ₹10L.

---

### §75(7) — Limitation for Passing Order
The proper officer must pass the order within a prescribed period after the SCN:

| Section | Order must be passed within |
|---|---|
| §73 (non-fraud) | **3 years** from due date of annual return |
| §74 (fraud) | **5 years** from due date of annual return |
| §74A (FY 2024-25+) | Within prescribed period |

If no DRC-07 order is passed within this period → proceedings lapse.

---

### §75(8) — No Double Jeopardy
Where an order is passed under §73 or §74, no further proceedings shall be initiated for the same facts and period.

**Use in reply:** If a second SCN is issued for the same period and same facts after a DRC-07 order → challenge as barred by §75(8).

---

### §75(9) — Erroneous Refund
Where any amount paid as refund is found to be erroneous, recovery shall be made under §73 or §74 as applicable.

---

### §75(10) — Self-Assessed Tax Recovery
Where a taxable person fails to pay self-assessed tax, recovery shall be made under §79 **without** issuing an SCN under §73/74.

**Important:** Officers sometimes issue §73 SCN for self-assessed but unpaid tax — raise objection that §75(10) requires recovery under §79 directly, not a fresh demand proceeding.

---

## Checklist — §75 Objections to Raise in DRC-06

- [ ] Was third-party information used without prior opportunity? → §75(1) violated
- [ ] Was personal hearing granted or offered? → §75(2)/(3) right
- [ ] Request personal hearing explicitly in DRC-06 → mandatory under §75(3)
- [ ] Is the DRC-07 order a speaking order? → §75(5) requirement
- [ ] Is penalty calculated on actual tax, not SCN amount? → §75(6)
- [ ] Is the order within the time limit? → §75(7)
- [ ] Is this a second SCN for same facts/period? → §75(8) bar

---

## Standard §75(3) Personal Hearing Request (Include in Every DRC-06)

> "The taxpayer hereby requests a personal hearing under Section 75(3) of the CGST Act, 2017 before any order is passed in the above matter. The taxpayer submits that a personal hearing is essential to explain the factual position and legal grounds in detail, and that passing an order without affording such hearing would be violative of the principles of natural justice and the express provisions of Section 75(3)."

---

## Key Case Law

- **Natural justice — personal hearing mandatory:** Asstt. Commissioner v. Shukla & Brothers (SC) — opportunity of hearing cannot be dispensed with in tax matters
- **Speaking order requirement:** Orders without reasons are non-est — multiple HC decisions
- **Penalty on actual tax, not SCN amount:** Several GST AAR / HC decisions affirming §75(6) literal reading
