# Section 17 — Apportionment of Credit and Blocked Credits

## Plain-English Summary

Section 17 has two distinct functions:
1. **§17(1)–(4)** — ITC apportionment rules (partial business use, mixed taxable/exempt supplies, banking companies)
2. **§17(5)** — **Blocked credits** — a hard negative list of goods/services on which ITC is categorically denied, regardless of business use

**§17(5) is where 90% of the action is** in GST notices. Officers use it to deny ITC on motor vehicles, food, club memberships, construction, and CSR expenses. The key battleground in every §17(5) dispute is whether the taxpayer's case falls within an **exception** to the block.

---

## §17(1) — Partial Business Use Apportionment

Where goods/services are used **partly for business and partly for other purposes**, ITC is restricted to the portion attributable to business use.

**Formula**: Credit = (Business use / Total use) × Total input tax

**Example**: A car used 60% for business and 40% for personal use — ITC limited to 60%.

**Agent alert**: If the officer alleges personal use of business goods/services, the taxpayer must demonstrate the business-use ratio with evidence (usage logs, business records, etc.).

---

## §17(2) — Taxable vs Exempt Supply Apportionment

Where goods/services are used partly for **taxable supplies** (including zero-rated) and partly for **exempt supplies**, ITC is restricted to the taxable portion.

**Formula**: Prescribed under Rule 42 (inputs/input services) and Rule 43 (capital goods).

**Key point**: Zero-rated supplies (exports, SEZ supplies) are treated as taxable for this purpose — full ITC is available on zero-rated supply inputs.

---

## §17(3) — What Counts as "Exempt Supply" for Apportionment

Exempt supply includes:
- Supplies exempt from GST
- Supplies under **reverse charge** (RCM) — the recipient pays tax, so the supplier's supply is treated as exempt for apportionment purposes
- **Transactions in securities**
- **Sale of land**
- **Sale of building** (subject to Schedule II para 5(b))

**Explanation**: Schedule III activities (neither supply of goods nor services) are **excluded** from exempt supply — EXCEPT for para 5 (land/building sale) and any prescribed transactions under para 8(a).

---

## §17(4) — Banking / NBFC Option

Banking companies and NBFCs can either:
- Follow the §17(2) apportionment formula, OR
- Avail **50% of eligible ITC** every month (balance lapses)

Once exercised, the option cannot be withdrawn during the financial year.

**Exception**: The 50% restriction does not apply to tax paid on supplies between **related parties with the same PAN** (intra-group transactions).

---

## §17(5) — Blocked Credits ⭐ The Most Important Sub-Section

**Overrides §16(1) and §18(1)** — even if goods/services are used for business, ITC is blocked if they fall in this list.

The blocked list has exceptions within it — these exceptions are the primary defence in §17(5) notices.

---

### Block (a) — Motor Vehicles (≤13 seats including driver)

**Blocked**: ITC on motor vehicles for transportation of persons with approved seating capacity of **13 or fewer persons** (including driver).

**Exceptions — ITC IS available if the vehicle is used for**:
- **(A)** Further supply (dealer selling motor vehicles)
- **(B)** Transportation of passengers (cab aggregators, bus operators, tour operators)
- **(C)** Imparting training on driving such vehicles (driving schools)

**Common disputes**:
- Company cars for directors/employees → Blocked (no exception)
- Cab services used for employee transportation → Blocked at employer level (employer is not in passenger transport business); if directly by cab operator → Available under exception (B)
- Vehicle for site visits / field work → Blocked (not one of the 3 exceptions)
- Ambulance → Check seating capacity; if ≤13, generally blocked unless patient transport counts as "transportation of persons"

---

### Block (aa) — Vessels and Aircraft

**Blocked**: ITC on vessels and aircraft.

**Exceptions — ITC IS available if used for**:
- Further supply (sale of vessels/aircraft)
- Transportation of passengers
- Imparting training on navigation / flying
- **Transportation of goods** (distinct from (a) — goods transport by vessel/aircraft is an exception)

---

### Block (ab) — Insurance, Servicing, Repair & Maintenance of (a)/(aa) Assets

**Blocked**: ITC on general insurance, servicing, repair and maintenance of motor vehicles/vessels/aircraft covered under (a) and (aa).

**Exceptions — ITC IS available if**:
- The underlying vehicle/vessel/aircraft is itself used for the exception purposes under (a)/(aa) — so if a cab company's vehicle is maintained, the maintenance ITC is available
- Received by a manufacturer of such motor vehicles/vessels/aircraft
- Received by an insurer insuring such assets

---

### Block (b) — Specific Services

#### (b)(i) — Food, Beverages & Lifestyle Services
**Blocked**:
- Food and beverages
- Outdoor catering
- Beauty treatment
- Health services
- Cosmetic and plastic surgery
- Leasing/renting/hiring of motor vehicles, vessels, aircraft covered under (a)/(aa)
- Life insurance
- Health insurance

**Exception**: ITC available if the **inward supply is used to make an outward taxable supply of the same category** OR as an element of a **taxable composite/mixed supply**.

**Examples**:
- Restaurant buying food ingredients → Available (outward supply of food = same category)
- Hotel providing catering services buying outdoor catering → Available
- Insurance company buying re-insurance → Available (same category)
- Pharma company buying health services for employees → Blocked (not making health services as output)
- Office canteen food → Blocked (employer not making taxable supply of food)

#### (b)(ii) — Club / Fitness Memberships
**Blocked**: Membership of a club, health and fitness centre.

**No exception** — fully blocked regardless of purpose.

**Common disputes**:
- Corporate gym memberships for employees → Blocked
- Club memberships for client entertainment → Blocked
- Sports club for employee welfare → Blocked

#### (b)(iii) — Travel Benefits to Employees
**Blocked**: Travel benefits to employees on vacation — leave travel concession (LTC), home travel concession (HTC).

**Exception**: Available if it is **obligatory for the employer** to provide such benefit under any law in force (e.g., statutory LTC under Central Government service rules for certain employees).

---

### Block (c) — Works Contract for Immovable Property Construction

**Blocked**: Works contract services for **construction of an immovable property** (other than plant and machinery).

**Exception**: ITC available if the works contract service is an **input service for further supply of works contract service** — i.e., a sub-contractor providing works contract to a main contractor who will in turn provide works contract services.

**"Construction" includes** (Explanation 1): re-construction, renovation, additions, alterations, repairs — **to the extent of capitalisation**. If the repair is expensed (not capitalized), this block does not apply.

**Key battleground — "plant and machinery" vs "plant or machinery"**:
- Explanation 2 (inserted by Finance Act 2021, retrospective): the reference is to **"plant and machinery"** as defined at the end of §17 — apparatus, equipment, machinery **fixed to earth** by foundation or structural support, used for making outward supply. Excludes: land, buildings, civil structures, telecom towers, pipelines outside factory.
- This overrides contrary court decisions that had read "plant or machinery" disjunctively.

**Common disputes**:
- Factory building construction → Blocked (immovable property; not plant and machinery)
- Machinery installation involving civil work → Partially available (civil component blocked, machinery component available if it qualifies as plant and machinery)
- Warehouse construction → Blocked
- Cold storage construction → Contested — if cold storage is the very apparatus for making taxable supply (e.g., frozen food), it may qualify as plant and machinery; strong judicial debate

---

### Block (d) — Goods/Services for Own-Account Construction of Immovable Property

**Blocked**: Goods or services received for **construction of an immovable property on own account** (other than plant and machinery), even if used in the course of business.

**"Construction" includes** (Explanation 1): same as (c) — re-construction, renovation, additions, alterations, repairs **to the extent of capitalisation**.

**Own account** = the taxpayer is constructing for himself (not for a customer). If constructing for a customer (as a contractor), §17(5)(c) applies instead.

**Common disputes**:
- Company constructing its own office building → Blocked
- Company constructing a building to rent out (renting is taxable) → Blocked — immovable property construction ITC blocked even if the output (rental income) is taxable; this is a harsh provision consistently upheld
- Renovation of own office → Blocked to the extent capitalised; if expensed as repair, check if capitalised
- Solar panels on factory roof → If fixed to structure and used for generating power for factory output, may qualify as plant and machinery — contested

---

### Block (e) — Composition Scheme Purchases

**Blocked**: Goods/services on which tax has been paid under **Section 10** (composition levy).

Composition dealers pay tax at a flat rate and cannot issue tax invoices — their customers cannot claim ITC.

---

### Block (f) — Non-Resident Taxable Person

**Blocked**: Goods/services received by a **non-resident taxable person**, except goods **imported by him**.

---

### Block (fa) — CSR Expenses (Inserted by Finance Act 2023)

**Blocked**: Goods/services used for activities relating to **Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR)** obligations under Section 135 of the Companies Act, 2013.

**This is a significant recent addition** — all ITC on CSR expenditure is blocked. This includes:
- CSR projects (school construction, health camps, etc.)
- CSR donations in kind
- Professional services for CSR compliance

**Agent alert**: If a company claimed ITC on CSR expenses incurred before the (fa) insertion, check the effective date. (fa) applies prospectively — check when the amendment took effect in the relevant state.

---

### Block (g) — Personal Consumption

**Blocked**: Goods/services used for **personal consumption**.

**Defence**: The goods/services were used entirely for business — personal use was incidental/nil. Produce business records showing the goods are in business use (asset registers, usage logs, business purpose documentation).

---

### Block (h) — Goods Lost, Stolen, Destroyed, Written Off, Gifts, Free Samples

**Blocked**: ITC on goods that are:
- Lost, stolen, destroyed, written off, OR
- Disposed of by way of **gift or free samples**

**Common disputes**:
- Free samples to customers → Blocked; any ITC availed must be reversed
- Goods damaged in warehouse → Blocked to extent written off; if insurance claim received, check if that changes anything
- Promotional items / gifts to employees → Blocked
- Destroyed goods with insurance payout → Still blocked (destruction triggers the block regardless of insurance)

---

### Block (i) — Tax Paid Under §74 (Fraud Cases, up to FY 2023-24)

**Blocked**: Any tax paid in accordance with **Section 74** (fraud/suppression) for periods up to FY 2023-24.

**Meaning**: If a demand was raised under §74 and the taxpayer paid it — no ITC is available on that payment. The penalty is that fraud-track payments do not generate ITC credit.

---

## "Plant and Machinery" — Statutory Definition (End of §17)

> "Plant and machinery" means **apparatus, equipment, and machinery fixed to earth by foundation or structural support** that are used for making outward supply of goods or services or both and includes such foundation and structural supports but **excludes**:
> - (i) Land, building or any other civil structures
> - (ii) Telecommunication towers
> - (iii) Pipelines laid outside the factory premises

**This definition applies across all of Chapter V** (ITC provisions).

**Agent alert**: When a client claims ITC on construction/works contract and argues it is "plant and machinery":
1. Is it fixed to earth by foundation or structural support? ✓
2. Is it used for making outward supply? ✓
3. Is it land/building/civil structure? If yes → excluded, blocked
4. Is it a telecom tower or pipeline outside factory? If yes → excluded, blocked

## Procedural Defects / Defence Framework

### Step 1 — Identify which block the officer is invoking
The SCN/ASMT-10 must specify which clause of §17(5) is being invoked. If the notice is vague ("ITC wrongly availed under §17(5)") without specifying the clause — raise the vagueness objection.

### Step 2 — Check if an exception applies
Every block under (a), (aa), (ab), (b)(i), (b)(iii), and (c) has exceptions. Match the taxpayer's activity to the exception.

### Step 3 — Check if "construction" is capitalized
For (c) and (d) — if the works/goods relate to repairs/maintenance that were **expensed** (not capitalized in the balance sheet), the "construction" block does not apply. Produce financial statements showing treatment.

### Step 4 — Plant and machinery argument
For immovable property blocks ((c) and (d)) — argue that the asset qualifies as "plant and machinery" under the §17 definition. Produce technical details: foundation/structural support, role in making taxable output.

### Step 5 — Prospective application of (fa)
For CSR — if the expenditure was incurred before (fa) was inserted, argue that the block does not apply retroactively.

## Key Case Law

### On works contract and immovable property — construction for rental income
**Safari Retreats Pvt. Ltd. v. Chief Commissioner of CGST (Orissa HC / SC)**
> Originally held by Orissa HC: ITC on construction of a mall (for renting out commercial spaces, which is taxable) should be available since the output is taxable. The SC referred the matter to a larger bench. The matter is pending — **do not rely on this as settled law.** The conservative position is that §17(5)(d) blocks ITC on own-account construction even for taxable rental output.

### On "plant and machinery" vs civil structure
**DSIDC v. Commissioner (Delhi HC)**
> Held: The definition of "plant and machinery" in §17 is specific and excludes buildings and civil structures. The taxpayer cannot claim ITC on civil construction work by labelling it as plant and machinery unless it strictly meets the statutory definition (fixed to earth, used for outward supply, not a building).

### On free samples — ITC reversal required
**CBIC Circular 92/11/2019-GST**
> Clarified: ITC availed on goods subsequently distributed as free samples must be reversed under §17(5)(h). The reversal should be done in GSTR-3B Table 4(B)(2).

### On food/beverages — canteen facility
**Tata Motors Ltd. v. State of Gujarat (Gujarat AAR)**
> Held: ITC on canteen services provided to factory workers is blocked under §17(5)(b)(i). Even if the canteen is a statutory obligation under the Factories Act, the exception under (b)(iii) covers only travel benefits — not food/canteen services.

### On "to the extent of capitalisation" — repair vs capital
**Consistent departmental and judicial position**
> The words "to the extent of capitalisation" in Explanation 1 to §17(5)(c)/(d) mean that only the portion of repair/renovation that is capitalised (added to the asset's book value) triggers the block. Revenue expenditure on repairs that is fully expensed does not attract the construction block, and ITC on such expenses is available.
