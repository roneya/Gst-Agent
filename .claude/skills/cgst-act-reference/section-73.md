# Section 73 — Determination of Tax (Non-Fraud, up to FY 2023-24)

## ⚠️ Applicability Warning — Read First

**Section 73 applies ONLY to periods up to FY 2023-24.**

From FY 2024-25 onwards, Section 73 has been replaced by **Section 74A** (inserted by Finance Act 2024). If the notice covers FY 2024-25 or later, load `section-74a.md` instead.

If the notice covers **both pre and post FY 2023-24 periods**, it should ideally be split — raise this as a preliminary objection if the officer has issued a single SCN mixing §73 and §74A periods.

---

## Plain-English Summary

Section 73 is the **primary demand provision** for non-fraud GST cases. When the tax department believes a taxpayer has:
- Not paid tax, OR
- Short-paid tax, OR
- Claimed an erroneous refund, OR
- Wrongly availed or utilised ITC

…and there is **no allegation of fraud, willful misstatement, or suppression of facts**, the officer issues a Show Cause Notice (SCN) under Section 73.

This is the most common demand notice in GST. The taxpayer gets a chance to reply, and if the officer agrees, the matter is closed. If not, a demand order (with 10% penalty) is issued.

The key advantages of §73 over §74:
- **Shorter limitation** (3 years vs 5 years)
- **Lower penalty** (10% vs 100%)
- **No fraud stigma**

---

## When This Section Applies

Load this skill when the SCN / notice states:
- "Show Cause Notice under Section 73"
- "Tax not paid / short paid for the period [FY 20XX-XX to FY 2023-24]"
- "ITC wrongly availed / utilised"
- "Erroneous refund sanctioned"
- The notice does **not** use words like "fraud", "suppression", "willful misstatement", "intent to evade"
- The form is **DRC-01** (SCN) or **DRC-07** (order)

If the notice cites **Section 74** instead, load `section-74.md` — that is the fraud track with 100% penalty and 5-year limitation.

## Sub-Section Breakdown

### §73(1) — The Show Cause Notice (SCN)
The proper officer **shall serve notice** on the person chargeable, requiring them to show cause why they should not pay:
- The **tax** shortfall
- **Interest** under Section 50
- **Penalty** under the Act

**Key words for the agent**:
- "Shall serve" — mandatory, not discretionary. If no SCN was served, the demand order is void.
- "Any reason other than fraud…" — the moment the officer suspects fraud, he must shift to §74 (higher threshold, higher penalty, longer limitation)
- The SCN must specify the **tax amount**, **period**, and **grounds**

---

### §73(2) — Minimum Gap Between SCN and Order
The SCN must be issued **at least 3 months before** the time limit for the order under §73(10).

**Why this matters**: If the SCN was issued less than 3 months before the §73(10) deadline, the SCN (and any resulting order) is procedurally defective. Raise this as a preliminary objection.

Example: If the §73(10) deadline is 31-Dec-2024, the SCN must have been issued by 30-Sep-2024 at the latest.

---

### §73(3) & §73(4) — Statement for Additional Periods
Instead of issuing a fresh SCN for every tax period, the officer may serve a **statement** covering additional periods — provided:
- A valid SCN already exists under §73(1) for at least one period, AND
- The **grounds are identical** to those in the original SCN

The statement is treated as a deemed SCN. If the grounds differ from the original SCN, the statement is invalid for those additional periods.

**Agent alert**: Check whether all periods in the statement share the same grounds as the original SCN. If not, object to the inclusion of periods with different grounds.

---

### §73(5) — Pre-SCN Voluntary Payment (Golden Window)
**Before** the SCN is served, the taxpayer may:
1. Pay the tax + interest under §50 on self-assessment or based on officer's informal intimation
2. Inform the officer **in writing** of such payment

Result: **No SCN. No penalty.** The officer cannot serve the notice for the amount paid.

**This is the best outcome** — pay before SCN, no SCN, no penalty, matter closed.

---

### §73(6) — Officer's Duty After Pre-SCN Payment
On receiving the information of voluntary payment, the officer **shall not** serve:
- The SCN under §73(1), OR
- The statement under §73(3)

…for the tax so paid. The officer's hands are tied — this is a mandatory prohibition, not discretionary.

---

### §73(7) — Shortfall After Pre-SCN Payment
If the officer believes the pre-SCN payment is **short** of the actual liability, he may issue an SCN only for the **shortfall** amount.

**Agent alert**: If this happens, the reply should argue that the taxpayer's self-assessment was correct and the officer's computation of shortfall is erroneous.

---

### §73(8) — Payment Within 30 Days of SCN = No Penalty
If the taxpayer pays **tax + interest within 30 days of the SCN**:
- **No penalty** is payable
- All proceedings are **deemed concluded**

This is the most important provision in §73 from a defence strategy standpoint.

**How to use**: If the tax demand has merit (partial or full), advise the client to pay within 30 days of SCN issuance and file a reply stating payment made. Quote the DRC-03 ARN. Officer must close proceedings.

**Exception**: See §73(11) — self-assessed tax not paid within 30 days of due date attracts penalty even under §73(8).

---

### §73(9) — The Demand Order
After considering the reply (if any), the officer determines:
- Tax due
- Interest under §50
- **Penalty = 10% of tax OR ₹10,000 — whichever is higher**

The order is issued in **Form DRC-07**.

**Note on penalty**: 10% is the minimum penalty under §73. It cannot be waived unless the taxpayer avails §73(8) (pay within 30 days of SCN) or §73(5) (pay before SCN).

---

### §73(10) — Limitation Period (3 Years) ⭐ Critical
The demand **order** must be issued within:
- **3 years** from the due date for filing the **annual return (GSTR-9)** for the financial year to which the short payment relates, OR
- **3 years** from the date of **erroneous refund** (for refund cases)

| Financial Year | GSTR-9 Due Date | §73(10) Order Deadline |
|---|---|---|
| FY 2017-18 | 31-Dec-2019 (extended) | 31-Dec-2022 |
| FY 2018-19 | 31-Aug-2020 (extended) | 31-Aug-2023 |
| FY 2019-20 | 28-Feb-2021 (extended) | 28-Feb-2024 |
| FY 2020-21 | 28-Feb-2022 | 28-Feb-2025 |
| FY 2021-22 | 31-Dec-2022 | 31-Dec-2025 |
| FY 2022-23 | 31-Dec-2023 | 31-Dec-2026 |
| FY 2023-24 | 31-Dec-2024 | 31-Dec-2027 |

**Agent alert**: Always check if the order deadline has passed. If yes, the demand is **time-barred** — the single strongest ground to contest.

---

### §73(11) — Exception: Self-Assessed Tax / Tax Collected but Not Paid
Even if §73(8) (30-day payment) is satisfied, **penalty is still payable** if:
- **Self-assessed tax** (declared in return but not paid) was not paid within **30 days from the due date of payment**, OR
- **Tax collected** from a customer was not remitted within 30 days

This is a carve-out that prevents misuse of §73(8) for deliberate non-payment of return-admitted liabilities.

**Agent alert**: If the SCN is for self-assessed tax default, the 30-day no-penalty window under §73(8) does NOT apply. Penalty is mandatory.

---

### §73(12) — Applicability Clause
Confirms: This section applies **only to periods up to FY 2023-24**.

| Event | Deadline |
|---|---|
| SCN must be issued before | At least 3 months before §73(10) order deadline |
| Pre-SCN voluntary payment | Any time before SCN is served |
| Payment after SCN to avoid penalty | Within **30 days** of SCN |
| Demand order | Within **3 years** from GSTR-9 due date of relevant FY |
| Reply to SCN | As specified in SCN (typically 15–30 days; extendable) |

## Penalty Structure Under Section 73

| Situation | Penalty |
|---|---|
| Pay before SCN (§73(5)) | **NIL** |
| Pay within 30 days of SCN (§73(8)) | **NIL** (subject to §73(11)) |
| Order passed after SCN (§73(9)) | **10% of tax or ₹10,000 — whichever is higher** |
| Self-assessed tax not paid within 30 days (§73(11)) | **Penalty mandatory** even if §73(8) satisfied |

## Critical Interpretive Points

### 1. The non-fraud threshold is crucial
§73 applies only when there is **no fraud, no willful misstatement, no suppression**. If the officer uses language like "deliberately", "intentionally", "concealed", or "suppressed" in the SCN but cites §73 — this is contradictory. Either:
- The officer must proceed under §74 (if fraud is alleged), or
- The language must be toned down and §73 is the correct provision

If the officer cites §73 but uses fraud-like language, challenge the notice for being internally inconsistent and argue §73 limitation applies (3 years, not 5).

### 2. §73 vs §74 — the officer cannot switch tracks mid-proceeding
Once an SCN is issued under §73, the officer **cannot convert it into a §74 proceeding** without issuing a fresh SCN under §74. Any order under §74 issued on a §73 SCN is void.

### 3. Statement under §73(3) cannot expand grounds
The grounds in the §73(3) statement must be **identical** to the §73(1) SCN. If the officer introduces a new allegation in the statement (not in the original SCN), that allegation is invalid for the additional periods — the taxpayer was never given opportunity to respond on that ground.

### 4. Interest under §50 is mandatory — but the rate matters
§73(1) mandates interest under §50. However:
- Rate is **18% p.a.** for tax not paid / short paid
- Rate is **24% p.a.** for ITC wrongly availed and utilised (not merely availed)
- No interest on **bonafide ITC claims** reversed before utilisation (contested — see case law)

### 5. The 30-day no-penalty window (§73(8)) is absolute — but check §73(11)
If the client can pay within 30 days of SCN, always recommend it. The only trap is §73(11) — self-assessed tax defaults where penalty cannot be avoided.

## Procedural Defects Checklist

Check the SCN for these defects before drafting the reply on merits:

| # | Defect | Ground |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | SCN issued after §73(10) limitation expired | Time-barred — demand is void |
| 2 | SCN issued less than 3 months before §73(10) deadline | Violates §73(2) — procedural defect |
| 3 | SCN does not specify tax amount / period / grounds | Vague SCN — taxpayer cannot reply meaningfully; violates natural justice |
| 4 | SCN issued by officer without jurisdiction | Void ab initio |
| 5 | SCN uses fraud/suppression language but cites §73 | Internal inconsistency — challenge the SCN |
| 6 | §73(3) statement introduces new grounds not in §73(1) SCN | Invalid for periods with new grounds |
| 7 | Same demand already adjudicated / pending in another forum | Res judicata / parallel proceedings |
| 8 | Pre-SCN payment already made under §73(5) — officer still served SCN | Officer violated §73(6) — mandatory prohibition |

## Defence Strategy Framework

### Step 1 — Classify the demand
Identify which of the 4 triggers applies:
- Tax not paid → verify return vs. challan
- Tax short paid → verify computation
- ITC wrongly availed → verify eligibility under §16 / §17(5)
- Erroneous refund → verify refund sanction order

### Step 2 — Check limitation first
Is the §73(10) deadline met? If order deadline has passed, this is your **primary ground** — the demand is time-barred regardless of merits.

### Step 3 — Check SCN timing
Was the SCN issued at least 3 months before the §73(10) deadline? (§73(2) compliance)

### Step 4 — Decide: pay or contest?
| If… | Then… |
|---|---|
| Demand is fully valid | Pay tax + interest within 30 days of SCN → no penalty → all proceedings concluded |
| Demand is partially valid | Pay admitted portion within 30 days + contest the rest |
| Demand is fully disputable | File detailed reply contesting all grounds |

### Step 5 — Draft the reply structure
1. **Preliminary Objections** (limitation, jurisdiction, procedural defects)
2. **Factual Background** (brief company profile, relevant transactions)
3. **Ground-wise Rebuttal** (address each allegation in the SCN)
4. **Legal Submissions** (statutory provisions, circulars, case law)
5. **Prayer** (quash/drop the SCN; in the alternative, reduce demand; no penalty under §73(8) if paying)

## Key Case Law

### On limitation (§73(10))
**State of Punjab v. Bhatinda District Cooperative Milk Producers Union Ltd. (SC)**
> Held: Limitation provisions in tax statutes are mandatory and go to the root of jurisdiction. A demand order passed after the limitation period is without jurisdiction and void — not merely voidable.

**Bharat Forge Ltd. v. Union of India (Bombay HC)**
> Held: The time limit under §73(10) runs from the due date of the annual return, not from the actual date of filing. Extensions granted for filing GSTR-9 do not automatically extend the §73(10) deadline unless a specific notification extends the order deadline.

---

### On SCN specificity — vague notices
**Oryx Fisheries Pvt. Ltd. v. Union of India (SC)**
> Held: A show cause notice must be precise and definite. If the notice does not clearly spell out the charges, it prejudices the taxpayer's right to reply and must be quashed.

**National Aluminium Company Ltd. v. Premsway Udyog (SC)**
> Held: The order cannot travel beyond the show cause notice. If the SCN alleges X, the order cannot demand Y.

---

### On §73 vs §74 — officer cannot switch tracks
**M/s Canon India Pvt. Ltd. v. Commissioner of Customs (SC)**
> Held: If the department has proceeded under the non-extended period provision (equivalent to §73), it cannot convert the proceeding to the extended period (equivalent to §74) after the SCN is issued. The test for invoking the extended period must be met at the time of SCN issuance.

---

### On ITC mismatch — GSTR-2A difference is not automatic disallowance
**Consistent line — Calcutta HC, Delhi HC, Madras HC (2021–2024)**
> Held: Section 16 does not require ITC claims to match GSTR-2A exactly. The taxpayer can prove the claim through invoice, proof of receipt of goods/services, and supplier's tax payment through independent evidence. GSTR-2A is a facilitative document, not the sole determinant. See `gst-common-defences/itc-mismatch.md`.

---

### On interest — ITC reversal before utilisation
**Union of India v. Bharti Airtel Ltd. (SC, 2022)**
> Held: Interest under §50 is chargeable only when ITC has been **wrongly utilised** (i.e., actually used to discharge output tax liability). Mere availment of ITC in the credit ledger without utilisation does not attract interest. However, note that §50(3) was amended prospectively — check whether the period in dispute is pre or post amendment.

## DRC-03 — How to Pay Under Section 73

### Pre-SCN payment (§73(5))
1. Go to GST portal → Services → Payments → Voluntary Tax Payment → **DRC-03**
2. Select financial year and tax head
3. Select cause: **"Voluntary"** or **"Audit/Scrutiny"** as applicable
4. Pay tax + interest (§50, 18% p.a.)
5. Write to the officer with DRC-03 ARN citing §73(5)
6. Officer cannot serve SCN for the amount paid

### Post-SCN payment within 30 days (§73(8))
1. File DRC-03 → cause: **"SCN"**
2. Select the SCN reference number
3. Pay tax + interest
4. File reply to SCN stating: "The Noticee has paid the tax and interest vide DRC-03 ARN [___] dated [___] within 30 days of the SCN. In view of §73(8), no penalty is payable and all proceedings stand concluded."
5. Request acknowledgment / closure from the officer

### Interest calculation
Interest = Tax amount × 18% ÷ 365 × number of days of delay (from due date of payment to actual payment date)
