# Section 74A — Determination of Tax for FY 2024-25 Onwards

## ⚠️ Applicability — Read First

**Section 74A applies ONLY to FY 2024-25 and onwards** (§74A(12)).

For periods up to FY 2023-24:
- Non-fraud → **Section 73** → load `section-73.md`
- Fraud → **Section 74** → load `section-74.md`

§74A is a **single unified provision** — it covers both fraud and non-fraud cases under one section. The distinction between fraud and non-fraud still matters for **penalty** and **settlement options**, but there is no separate provision for each track anymore.

---

## What Changed from §73/§74 — Key Differences at a Glance

| Feature | §73 (up to FY 2023-24) | §74 (up to FY 2023-24) | **§74A (FY 2024-25 onwards)** |
|---|---|---|---|
| Fraud/non-fraud | Separate sections | Separate sections | **Single unified section** |
| SCN limitation | 3 years from GSTR-9 due date (for order) | 5 years from GSTR-9 due date (for order) | **42 months from GSTR-9 due date (for SCN)** |
| Order deadline | Same as SCN deadline | Same as SCN deadline | **12 months from SCN** (extendable by 6 months) |
| Minimum threshold | None | None | **₹1,000** — no notice below this |
| Post-SCN payment window | 30 days | 30 days | **60 days** |
| Non-fraud penalty (order) | 10% or ₹10,000 | — | **10% or ₹10,000** |
| Fraud penalty (order) | — | 100% | **100%** |
| Pre-SCN non-fraud | Tax + interest, no penalty | — | **Tax + interest, no penalty** |
| Pre-SCN fraud | — | Tax + interest + 15% | **Tax + interest + 15%** |
| Post-SCN non-fraud (60 days) | Tax + interest (30 days) | — | **Tax + interest, no penalty** |
| Post-SCN fraud (60 days) | — | Tax + interest + 25% (30 days) | **Tax + interest + 25%** |
| Post-order fraud (60 days) | — | Tax + interest + 50% (30 days) | **Tax + interest + 50%** |

---

## Plain-English Summary

§74A streamlines the GST demand process for FY 2024-25 onwards by:
1. **Merging** §73 and §74 into one provision
2. **Restructuring timelines** — SCN must be issued within 42 months; order must follow within 12 months of SCN
3. **Adding a ₹1,000 minimum** — petty demands cannot be raised
4. **Extending the payment window** from 30 to **60 days**

The fraud/non-fraud distinction is preserved only for **penalty** — the officer uses the same SCN form regardless of whether fraud is alleged, but must specify which track applies for penalty purposes.

---

## §74A(1) — The Show Cause Notice

The proper officer shall serve notice when:
- Tax has not been paid or short paid, OR
- Erroneous refund has been granted, OR
- ITC has been wrongly availed or utilised

**For any reason** — unlike §73 (which excluded fraud) or §74 (which required fraud), §74A covers ALL reasons in one notice.

**Proviso — ₹1,000 minimum threshold**: No notice shall be issued if the tax not paid / short paid / ITC wrongly availed or utilised in a **financial year** is less than **₹1,000**.

**Agent alert**: Check the total demand for the financial year. If it is below ₹1,000, the notice itself is invalid and must be challenged.

---

## §74A(2) — SCN Limitation (42 Months) ⭐ Critical

The SCN must be issued within **42 months** from:
- The **due date for furnishing GSTR-9** for the financial year to which the demand relates, OR
- The **date of erroneous refund**

**Important structural change from §73/§74**: Under the old provisions, the limitation was for the **order**. Under §74A, the limitation is for the **SCN**. The order has its own separate deadline (§74A(7) — 12 months from SCN).

### §74A(2) SCN deadlines

| FY | GSTR-9 Due Date | 42-Month SCN Deadline |
|---|---|---|
| FY 2024-25 | 31-Dec-2025 | 30-Jun-2029 |
| FY 2025-26 | 31-Dec-2026 | 30-Jun-2030 |

**Agent alert**: If the SCN was issued after 42 months from the GSTR-9 due date, the entire proceeding is time-barred. This is the strongest ground to challenge.

---

## §74A(3) & §74A(4) — Statement for Additional Periods

Same as §73(3)/(4) — officer can serve a **statement** for additional periods instead of fresh SCN, provided the **grounds are identical** to those in the original §74A(1) SCN.

If the grounds differ, the statement is invalid for those additional periods.

---

## §74A(5) — Penalty Structure

The penalty in the **order** (§74A(6)) depends on the reason:

| Reason | Penalty |
|---|---|
| **Non-fraud** (any reason other than fraud/misstatement/suppression) | **10% of tax or ₹10,000 — whichever is higher** |
| **Fraud** (fraud/willful misstatement/suppression to evade tax) | **100% of tax** |

**Agent alert**: The SCN must clearly specify whether it is a fraud or non-fraud case. If the SCN uses fraud language but the facts do not support it, challenge the fraud classification and argue for non-fraud penalty (10%). The same challenge strategies from §74 apply here — see `section-74.md` for the full fraud-challenge playbook.

---

## §74A(6) — The Demand Order

After considering the taxpayer's reply, the officer issues the order determining:
- Tax due
- Interest under §50
- Penalty under §74A(5) (10% or 100% depending on fraud/non-fraud)

---

## §74A(7) — Order Deadline (12 Months from SCN) ⭐ New Two-Stage Timeline

The order must be issued within **12 months from the date of the SCN** (§74A(2) SCN).

**Extension**: The Commissioner (or officer senior to proper officer, at minimum Joint Commissioner level) may extend by a further **6 months** — but only:
- Before the 12-month period expires
- With reasons recorded in writing

**Maximum total timeline**: 42 months (SCN) + 12 months (order) + 6 months (extension) = **60 months from GSTR-9 due date** in the worst case.

### Two-stage timeline comparison

| Stage | §73 | §74 | §74A |
|---|---|---|---|
| SCN deadline | 3 years (same as order) | 5 years (same as order) | **42 months from GSTR-9 due date** |
| Order deadline | 3 years from GSTR-9 due date | 5 years from GSTR-9 due date | **12 months from SCN date** |
| Order extension | None | None | **6 months (Commissioner approval)** |

**Agent alert**: Under §74A, if the SCN is issued on Day 1, the order must follow by Month 12 (or Month 18 with extension). If the order is delayed beyond this, it is barred — even if the SCN was timely. Always check the SCN date vs. the order date.

---

## §74A(8) — Non-Fraud Settlement Options

For cases where the demand is **not** for fraud / willful misstatement / suppression:

### (i) Pre-SCN voluntary payment — Zero penalty
Pay: **Tax + interest (§50)** on own ascertainment or as ascertained by the officer, and inform the officer in writing.

Result: Officer **shall not** serve SCN for the amount paid. No penalty.

### (ii) Payment within 60 days of SCN — Zero penalty
Pay: **Tax + interest (§50)** within **60 days** of SCN.

Result: **No penalty. All proceedings deemed concluded.**

**Key change from §73**: The window is **60 days** (not 30 days). This gives more time to arrange funds.

---

## §74A(9) — Fraud Settlement Options

For cases where the demand is for **fraud / willful misstatement / suppression to evade tax**:

### (i) Pre-SCN voluntary payment — 15% penalty
Pay: **Tax + interest + 15% penalty** on own ascertainment, and inform the officer in writing.

Result: Officer **shall not** serve SCN for the amount paid.

### (ii) Payment within 60 days of SCN — 25% penalty
Pay: **Tax + interest + 25% penalty** within **60 days** of SCN.

Result: All proceedings deemed concluded.

### (iii) Payment within 60 days of order — 50% penalty
Pay: **Tax + interest + 50% penalty** within **60 days** of the order (DRC-07).

Result: All proceedings deemed concluded.

---

## §74A(10) — Shortfall After Pre-SCN Payment

If the officer believes the pre-SCN payment under §74A(8)(i) or §74A(9)(i) is short of the actual liability, the officer may issue SCN only for the shortfall.

---

## §74A(11) — Self-Assessed Tax Exception

**Even if §74A(8)(i) or (ii) is satisfied**, penalty is still payable if **self-assessed tax** or **tax collected** was not paid within **30 days from the due date**.

This mirrors §73(11) — the 60-day no-penalty window does not protect deliberate non-payment of return-admitted liabilities.

---

## §74A(12) — Applicability

This section applies to FY 2024-25 and onwards.

---

## Explanation 1 — "All proceedings" excludes §132

Settlement under §74A does not bar **criminal prosecution under §132**. The civil proceedings close but criminal exposure remains for fraud cases.

Co-noticees: Once the main person's proceedings are concluded under §74A, proceedings against co-noticees under §122/§125 are also deemed concluded (except §132).

---

## Explanation 2 — "Suppression" (Same as §74)

> "Suppression" means non-declaration of facts or information which a taxable person is required to declare in returns/statements/reports, OR failure to furnish information on being asked for in writing by the proper officer.

**Same standard as §74** — requires deliberate non-disclosure, not mere inadvertence. The full fraud-challenge playbook from `section-74.md` applies here too.

## Settlement Strategy — Which Window to Use

### Non-fraud cases

| When | Pay | Penalty | Result |
|---|---|---|---|
| Before SCN (§74A(8)(i)) | Tax + interest | **NIL** | No SCN served |
| Within 60 days of SCN (§74A(8)(ii)) | Tax + interest | **NIL** | Proceedings concluded |
| After order (contested) | Tax + interest + 10% | **10%** | Proceedings concluded |

### Fraud cases

| When | Pay | Penalty | Result |
|---|---|---|---|
| Before SCN (§74A(9)(i)) | Tax + interest | **15%** | No SCN served |
| Within 60 days of SCN (§74A(9)(ii)) | Tax + interest | **25%** | Proceedings concluded |
| Within 60 days of order (§74A(9)(iii)) | Tax + interest | **50%** | Proceedings concluded |
| After 60 days of order | Tax + interest | **100%** | — |

## Procedural Defects Checklist

| # | Defect | Ground |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Demand for the FY is less than ₹1,000 | Proviso to §74A(1) — notice invalid |
| 2 | SCN issued after 42 months from GSTR-9 due date | §74A(2) — time-barred |
| 3 | Order issued more than 12 months after SCN (without extension) | §74A(7) — order barred |
| 4 | Order extension granted beyond 6 months | §74A(7) proviso — excess extension invalid |
| 5 | Extension granted after the 12-month deadline expired | Extension must be granted **before** expiry |
| 6 | Extension granted by officer below Joint Commissioner rank | Invalid — must be Joint Commissioner or above |
| 7 | Fraud alleged but facts don't support it | Challenge fraud classification; argue non-fraud penalty (10%) |
| 8 | §74A(3) statement introduces new grounds | Invalid for those periods |
| 9 | SCN does not specify fraud or non-fraud track | Ambiguous notice — challenge specificity |

## How §74A Interacts with §73/§74 for Cross-Period Demands

If a single notice covers periods **both before and after FY 2023-24**:
- Pre-FY 2024-25 periods → governed by §73 or §74 (depending on fraud allegation)
- FY 2024-25 onwards → governed by §74A

The officer should ideally issue **separate notices** for each set of periods. A single notice mixing §73/§74 and §74A periods is procedurally questionable — raise this as a preliminary objection.

## DRC-03 — How to Pay Under §74A

### Pre-SCN non-fraud (§74A(8)(i))
- Portal → DRC-03 → cause: **"Voluntary"**
- Pay: tax + interest
- Inform officer in writing with ARN

### Pre-SCN fraud (§74A(9)(i))
- Portal → DRC-03 → cause: **"Voluntary"**
- Pay: tax + interest + **15% penalty**
- Inform officer in writing with ARN

### Post-SCN non-fraud within 60 days (§74A(8)(ii))
- Portal → DRC-03 → cause: **"SCN"** → select SCN reference
- Pay: tax + interest
- File reply stating payment and cite §74A(8)(ii)

### Post-SCN fraud within 60 days (§74A(9)(ii))
- Portal → DRC-03 → cause: **"SCN"** → select SCN reference
- Pay: tax + interest + **25% penalty**

### Post-order fraud within 60 days (§74A(9)(iii))
- Portal → DRC-03 → cause: **"Order"** → select DRC-07 reference
- Pay: tax + interest + **50% penalty**

## Key Interpretive Issues (New Law — Limited Case Law)

Since §74A applies from FY 2024-25, there is **no High Court or Supreme Court jurisprudence yet**. The following are anticipated disputes:

### 1. Does the 42-month SCN deadline run from GSTR-9 due date or actual filing date?
**Likely position**: From the **due date** (as with §73/§74) — not from the actual filing date. Extensions to GSTR-9 due dates by notification may affect this.

### 2. Can the officer issue both a §74A notice and a §65 audit for the same period?
**Anticipated**: Same principles as §73/§74 parallel proceedings apply — one should be stayed while the other proceeds.

### 3. Is the 12-month order deadline strictly jurisdictional?
**Anticipated**: Yes — similar to the limitation in §73/§74, a delayed order after the 12-month (or 18-month) window should be without jurisdiction and void.

### 4. What if the SCN is silent on fraud/non-fraud?
**Anticipated**: The officer must specify the track (fraud or non-fraud) in the SCN because the penalty consequences differ. A silent SCN may be challenged as ambiguous — the taxpayer cannot know whether to contest on merits or settle at 10%/25%.
