# Section 107 — Appeals to Appellate Authority

## When Does Section 107 Apply?

Section 107 is the **first level of appeal** in the GST demand lifecycle. Use this after:
- A DRC-07 order is passed confirming the demand (full or partial)
- An ASMT-13 best judgement assessment order is passed
- Any other order under the CGST Act that the taxpayer wants to contest

---

## Appeal Lifecycle

```
DRC-07 Order (adjudication by proper officer)
    ↓
APL-01 — Appeal to Appellate Authority (§107) — within 3 months
    ↓
APL-02 — Appellate Authority Acknowledgement
    ↓
Personal Hearing before Appellate Authority
    ↓
APL-04 — Appellate Order (confirm / modify / set aside)
    ↓ (if still aggrieved)
APL-05 — Appeal to Appellate Tribunal (GSTAT) under §112
    ↓
High Court (substantial question of law only) under §117
    ↓
Supreme Court under §118
```

---

## Key Provisions of Section 107

### §107(1) — Right to Appeal
Any person aggrieved by any decision or order passed under the CGST Act by an adjudicating authority may appeal to the Appellate Authority within **3 months** from the date of communication of such decision or order.

### §107(2) — Commissioner's Appeal
The Commissioner may also direct any officer to appeal against an order passed by the adjudicating authority within **6 months**.

### §107(4) — Extension of Time
The Appellate Authority may condone delay of up to **1 month** beyond the 3-month period if sufficient cause is shown.

### §107(6) — Pre-Deposit (Mandatory)
The taxpayer **must deposit** the following before the appeal is admitted:
- **10% of the disputed tax amount** (in addition to any admitted liability already paid)
- Pre-deposit is paid from cash ledger or credit ledger

> No stay of demand is possible without this pre-deposit.

### §107(7) — Stay of Recovery
On filing the appeal and making pre-deposit, **recovery of the balance demand is stayed** automatically until the appeal is decided.

### §107(8) — Time Limit for Decision
The Appellate Authority shall pass the order within **1 year** from the date of filing the appeal (extendable for sufficient cause).

### §107(11) — Appellate Order
The Appellate Authority may:
- Confirm, modify, or annul the order appealed against
- Remand the matter back to the adjudicating authority
- Cannot enhance the demand beyond what was in the original SCN

---

## Pre-Deposit Calculation

```
Pre-deposit = 10% of disputed tax

Note: Disputed tax = Total demand in DRC-07 minus admitted and paid amounts
```

**Example:**
- DRC-07 confirms ₹20,00,000 tax
- Taxpayer already paid ₹5,00,000 via DRC-03 before appeal
- Disputed amount = ₹15,00,000
- Pre-deposit = 10% × ₹15,00,000 = **₹1,50,000**

## How to File APL-01

### Documents Required
1. Copy of DRC-07 order being appealed
2. Statement of facts
3. Grounds of appeal
4. Evidence / documents supporting the appeal
5. Pre-deposit receipt (DRC-03 ARN or cash ledger payment)

### Portal Filing Path
```
Services → User Services → My Applications → Appeal to Appellate Authority
→ Select APL-01 → Upload DRC-07 order → Enter grounds → Pay pre-deposit → Submit
```

### Filing Deadline
- **3 months** from date of communication of DRC-07 order
- Date of communication = date of upload on portal (usually)
- Apply for condonation of delay (§107(4)) if beyond 3 months

---

## Grounds of Appeal — What to Raise

### Ground 1 — Factual Errors in DRC-07 Order
- Officer ignored documents submitted in DRC-06
- Demand figures are mathematically incorrect
- Wrong period / wrong GSTIN

### Ground 2 — Violation of Natural Justice
- No personal hearing granted despite request under §75(3)
- DRC-06 reply not considered
- Non-speaking order — no reasons recorded (§75(5))

### Ground 3 — Legal Errors
- Wrong section applied (§74 invoked for non-fraud case)
- Limitation period exceeded
- ITC denial contrary to §16(2) / case law
- Penalty exceeds maximum prescribed under §73

### Ground 4 — Time-Barred Order
- DRC-07 order passed beyond the period under §75(7)

### Ground 5 — New Grounds Not in SCN
- Officer confirmed demand on grounds not raised in the original SCN
- SCN cannot be expanded at DRC-07 stage

---

## Stay of Demand After Appeal

Once APL-01 is filed with pre-deposit:
- Recovery of the balance demand is **automatically stayed** under §107(7)
- No attachment of bank account / property during pendency of appeal
- If department still initiates recovery → file writ petition in HC immediately

## Key Case Law

- **Pre-deposit is mandatory:** Simplicity Industries v. UOI — no waiver of pre-deposit
- **Stay of demand on appeal filing:** §107(7) is automatic — department cannot recover pending appeal
- **Enhancement of demand in appeal:** Appellate Authority cannot enhance beyond SCN — §107(11)
- **Non-speaking order:** Set aside by multiple HCs — reasons must be recorded in DRC-07
