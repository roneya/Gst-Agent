# Section 65 — Audit by Tax Authorities

## What Is Section 65 Audit?

Section 65 empowers the **Commissioner or any officer authorised by him** to conduct an audit of a registered person's books of accounts and records.

This is **different from scrutiny (§61)** — an audit is a deeper examination of books, records, invoices, and financial statements, not just a comparison of return figures.

---

## Key Features

| Feature | Detail |
|---|---|
| Authority | Commissioner or authorised officer |
| Notice form | **ADT-01** |
| Minimum notice period | **15 working days** before audit commences |
| Duration | Completed within **3 months** from commencement |
| Extension | Commissioner may extend by a further 6 months |
| Audit report | **ADT-02** — communicated to taxpayer |
| Frequency | No restriction on frequency in law |

---

## Audit vs Scrutiny — Key Difference

| Aspect | §61 Scrutiny (ASMT-10) | §65 Audit (ADT-01) |
|---|---|---|
| Trigger | Return mismatch, data comparison | Commissioner's order |
| Scope | Specific discrepancy in returns | Full books + records examination |
| Duration | No fixed timeline | 3 months (extendable) |
| Notice | ASMT-10 | ADT-01 |
| Findings | ASMT-12 closure or §73/74 SCN | ADT-02 report + §73/74 SCN |
| Physical presence | Usually correspondence-based | Officer visits premises |

---

## Procedure Under Section 65

```
Step 1: Commissioner issues authorisation to audit officer
Step 2: ADT-01 notice issued to taxpayer (15 working days before)
Step 3: Audit commences — officer examines books, records, invoices
Step 4: Officer may call for additional documents / information
Step 5: Draft findings communicated to taxpayer
Step 6: Taxpayer given opportunity to respond to findings
Step 7: ADT-02 (audit report) issued with final findings
Step 8: If discrepancy found → SCN under §73 / §74 / §74A
```

---

## Taxpayer's Rights During Audit

### Right 1 — Minimum 15 Working Days Notice
ADT-01 must be served at least 15 working days before the audit. If less notice given → raise objection, request postponement.

### Right 2 — Opportunity to Explain
Before the final ADT-02 report is issued, the taxpayer must be given an opportunity to respond to the audit findings. If denied → violation of natural justice.

### Right 3 — Presence of CA / Advocate
Taxpayer may be represented by a CA, CMA, or advocate during audit proceedings.

### Right 4 — Dispute Audit Findings
The taxpayer can dispute the findings in writing before ADT-02 is finalised. Strong written representations at this stage can prevent SCNs from being issued.

### Right 5 — SCN Must Follow If Demand
Even after ADT-02, the officer cannot recover tax directly. A formal SCN under §73/74/74A must be issued and the taxpayer gets another opportunity to reply via DRC-06.

---

## Immediate Checklist on Receiving ADT-01

1. **Check notice period** — Is the 15-working-day minimum met?
2. **Note audit start date** — Organise records accordingly
3. **Identify the scope** — Which FYs? Which taxes? Specific allegation or general?
4. **Appoint CA / advocate** — Engage professional immediately for audit support
5. **Organise records** — Purchase register, sales register, ITC register, GSTR-1/3B, GSTR-9, bank statements, e-way bills, stock records
6. **Check for outstanding returns** — File any pending returns before audit commences

---

## Documents Usually Examined in §65 Audit

| Document | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Purchase invoices | Verify ITC eligibility |
| Sales invoices | Verify output tax |
| Books of accounts (Tally / ERP data) | Cross-check with returns |
| Bank statements | Verify payment receipts, supplier payments |
| Stock records | Verify opening/closing stock, wastage |
| E-way bills | Verify movement of goods |
| GSTR-1 / GSTR-3B / GSTR-9 | Return vs books comparison |
| Import / export documents | For importers/exporters |
| Contracts / agreements | For service providers |

## Responding to ADT-02 Audit Report

When the officer communicates draft findings before issuing ADT-02:

1. Read each finding carefully
2. Prepare a written rebuttal for every finding — facts + documents + legal ground
3. File the rebuttal promptly (before ADT-02 is finalised)
4. Request a personal meeting / hearing with the audit officer

For each finding:
- **If correct** — Quantify and pay via DRC-03 voluntarily before SCN (saves penalty under §73/§74A)
- **If incorrect** — Rebut with documents + case law

---

## After ADT-02 — SCN Stage

Once ADT-02 is issued and the officer decides to raise a demand:
- SCN will be issued in **DRC-01** under §73/74/74A
- Load `drc-01-scn.md` and `drc-06-reply.md` for the reply strategy
- The audit findings become the basis of the SCN — prepare point-wise rebuttal

---

## Key Legal Points

1. **Audit ≠ Final demand** — ADT-02 is a report, not a demand. Tax can only be recovered after SCN + order.

2. **Audit findings can be challenged** — Both at the DRC-06 stage and in appeal under §107.

3. **Voluntary payment before SCN** — If audit reveals genuine defaults, pay via DRC-03 before SCN is issued → nil/reduced penalty under §73(5).

4. **Section 65 audit period** — Cannot cover periods already covered by another §65 audit (no double jeopardy for same period/facts under §75(8)).

---

## Key Case Law

- *Pradeep Goyal v. UOI* — Natural justice applies; taxpayer must get opportunity before ADT-02 is finalised
- *Orient Craft Ltd* — Audit findings cannot be treated as final tax demand without SCN
- *M/s Suresh Kumar & Co.* — 15-day notice requirement is mandatory, not directory
