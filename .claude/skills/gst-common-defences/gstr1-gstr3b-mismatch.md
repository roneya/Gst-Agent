# GSTR-1 vs GSTR-3B Turnover Mismatch Defence

## The Allegation
Officer claims: "Turnover in GSTR-1 (₹X) is higher than GSTR-3B (₹Y) — differential tax of ₹Z not paid."

## Common Legitimate Reasons for Mismatch (Use as applicable)

| Reason | Explanation |
|---|---|
| **Credit notes issued** | GSTR-1 shows gross invoices; GSTR-3B reflects net (after credit notes). Credit note register + GSTR-1 Table 9B |
| **Advances received** | Advance receipt reported in GSTR-1 Table 11A; tax paid in GSTR-3B when invoice raised later |
| **Timing difference** | Invoice in GSTR-1 of Month A; tax paid in GSTR-3B of Month B (within same FY) |
| **Nil-rated / exempt supplies** | Reported in GSTR-1 but no tax payable — hence lower GSTR-3B tax |
| **Zero-rated supplies (exports)** | Appear in GSTR-1 but tax refunded/bond — not in GSTR-3B taxable turnover |
| **RCM supplies** | Reported in GSTR-1 as outward supply but tax paid by recipient under RCM — not in GSTR-3B outward tax |
| **Amendments** | Amendments in GSTR-1 Table 9A/9B creating apparent mismatch |
| **Data entry error** | Specific invoice duplicated in GSTR-1 — produce books to show actual supply |

## Reply Steps

1. Prepare a **month-wise reconciliation**: GSTR-1 turnover vs GSTR-3B turnover vs books of accounts
2. Identify the exact invoices causing the difference
3. For each — assign one of the above reasons
4. Attach: Sales register, credit note register, export proof (Shipping Bill), advance receipt register
5. Show that net tax liability = GSTR-3B tax paid (i.e., no actual short payment)

## Key Legal Point
GSTR-1 and GSTR-3B serve different purposes. GSTR-1 reports invoice-level outward supply details; GSTR-3B reports net tax liability after adjustments. A mismatch does not automatically mean tax evasion — the officer must prove actual short payment after considering all adjustments.
