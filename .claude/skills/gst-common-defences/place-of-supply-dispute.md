# Place of Supply Disputes — Defence Playbook

## Why Place of Supply Matters

The place of supply determines whether a transaction is:
- **Intra-state** → CGST + SGST applies
- **Inter-state** → IGST applies

A wrong determination leads to:
- Wrong tax type charged (IGST instead of CGST+SGST or vice versa)
- ITC mismatch at the recipient's end
- SCN for short payment / wrong payment of tax

| Provision | Subject |
|---|---|

| Section 11 | Place of supply — goods (imports / exports) |
| Section 12 | Place of supply — services (B2C / recipient in India) |
| Section 13 | Place of supply — services (involving foreign party) |

## Common SCN Scenarios

### Scenario 1 — IGST Charged on Intra-State Supply
**Officer's allegation:** Supplier charged IGST but supplier and recipient are in the same state → should have been CGST+SGST.

**Defence:**
- Verify supplier's GSTIN state vs recipient's GSTIN state
- If it is genuinely intra-state → accept, pay CGST+SGST, claim credit for wrongly paid IGST (IGST can be used as credit for CGST+SGST liability)
- If the supply is inter-state (e.g., bill-to ship-to scenario) → argue §10(1)(b) — place of supply is where the goods are delivered

### Scenario 2 — CGST+SGST Charged on Inter-State Supply
**Officer's allegation:** Supplier charged CGST+SGST but supply was inter-state → should have been IGST.

**Defence:**
- Verify the actual place of delivery of goods
- For services: verify location of service provider vs location of service recipient
- If genuinely inter-state → pay IGST, claim credit for CGST+SGST wrongly paid
- Note: CGST+SGST paid in error cannot directly offset IGST liability — separate payment needed

### Scenario 3 — B2B Treated as B2C (or Vice Versa)
**Officer's allegation:** Invoice issued as B2C (without buyer GSTIN) but buyer is GST-registered; or invoice shows unregistered buyer but buyer was registered.

**Defence:**
- If buyer was registered but not quoted on invoice → obtain buyer's GSTIN confirmation, issue credit note and revised invoice
- Revenue impact: Output tax amount same, but ITC at buyer's end affected
- Argue: No revenue loss to government — tax was paid, only invoice format was incorrect

## Place of Supply — Goods (Section 10)

| Transaction | Place of Supply |
|---|---|
| Movement of goods — domestic | Where movement terminates (place of delivery) |
| No movement of goods | Location of goods at time of supply |
| Goods assembled at site | Location of installation |
| Goods on board a conveyance | Location where goods are taken on board |
| **Bill-to Ship-to** | Location of recipient (person on whose direction goods delivered) |

**Bill-to Ship-to defence:** If goods are billed to Party A but delivered to Party B (in another state) — the place of supply is Party B's state (interstate), not Party A's state. IGST applies, not CGST+SGST.

---

## Place of Supply — Services (Section 12 / 13)

### Key Rules for Common Services

| Service | Place of Supply |
|---|---|
| Services to registered person (B2B) | Location of the registered recipient |
| Services to unregistered person (B2C) | Location of the recipient (if address available) else location of supplier |
| Immovable property services (construction, architecture, real estate) | Location of immovable property |
| Restaurant / catering | Where the service is actually performed |
| Transportation of goods (domestic) | Location of the recipient (if registered) |
| Training and performance appraisal (B2B) | Location of the registered recipient |
| Online information services (to unregistered) | Location of the recipient |

## Common Defence — Revenue-Neutral Argument

When wrong tax type was paid (IGST instead of CGST+SGST or vice versa):
> "The total quantum of tax paid to the government is identical regardless of whether it is classified as IGST or CGST+SGST. The government has not suffered any revenue loss. The error is merely one of classification of the tax head, not of evasion. The tax was paid in a government account — either the consolidated fund (IGST) or the respective state/central fund (CGST+SGST)."

*Cite: Commissioner of Central Excise v. Sarvesh Refractories (SC) — technical irregularities without revenue loss should not attract penalty*

## How to Rectify Wrong POS Determination

### Option A — Issue Credit Note + Revised Invoice
- Issue credit note for original invoice (reversing the wrong tax type)
- Issue fresh invoice with correct tax type
- File amendment in GSTR-1 (Table 9 / Table 10)
- Adjust in GSTR-3B

### Option B — Pay the Correct Tax, Claim Refund of Incorrect Tax
- Pay IGST (if that was correct) via DRC-03
- Apply for refund of wrongly paid CGST+SGST under §77 of CGST Act
- §77 specifically provides for refund of tax paid under wrong head

---

## Key Provisions for Wrong Head Payment

- **Section 77 CGST Act** — Refund of tax paid under wrong head (intra-state paid as inter-state)
- **Section 19 IGST Act** — Refund of tax paid as IGST when CGST+SGST should have been paid
