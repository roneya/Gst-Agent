# Blocked Credit — Section 17(5) Defence Playbook

## What Is Section 17(5)?

Section 17(5) lists specific categories of goods and services on which **ITC is blocked** — i.e., the registered person cannot claim ITC even if all conditions of §16(2) are met.

Officers frequently issue SCNs alleging that ITC was claimed on blocked items. This playbook covers the most common disputes and how to contest them.

---

## Complete List of Blocked Credits Under §17(5)

| Category | Blocked? | Exceptions (ITC allowed) |
|---|---|---|
| Motor vehicles for transport of persons (< 13 seats) | **Blocked** | If used for: further supply of vehicles, transport of passengers, imparting training on driving |
| Motor vehicles for transport of goods | **NOT blocked** | ITC fully available |
| Vessels and aircraft | **Blocked** | If used for transport of passengers / further supply / training |
| Food and beverages, outdoor catering | **Blocked** | If used to make an outward taxable supply of the same category |
| Beauty treatment, health services, cosmetic surgery | **Blocked** | If used to make outward taxable supply of same |
| Membership of clubs / health / fitness centres | **Blocked** | No exception |
| Travel benefits to employees (leave / home travel) | **Blocked** | No exception |
| Life insurance / health insurance | **Blocked** | If mandated by law (ESIC, group health as per govt notification) |
| Works contract services — for construction of immovable property | **Blocked** | Except if used for further supply of works contract |
| Goods / services for construction of immovable property (own account) | **Blocked** | Except if plant and machinery |
| Goods lost, stolen, destroyed, written off, gifted, free samples | **Blocked** | No exception |
| Tax paid on composition scheme, §10 | **Blocked** | No exception |

---

## Key Definitions — What Is "Plant and Machinery"?

**Plant and Machinery** (Explanation to §17) = Apparatus, equipment, machinery fixed to earth by foundation or structural support used for making outward supply. **Excludes:** land, buildings, civil structures, telecommunication towers, pipelines.

ITC on plant and machinery is **NOT blocked** even under works contract or construction — this is a major exception officers often ignore.

## Common SCN Grounds and Defences

### Ground 1 — ITC on Motor Vehicles Disallowed

**Officer's position:** Vehicles purchased — ITC blocked under §17(5)(a).

**Defence:**
- **Check the vehicle type** — Vehicles for transport of goods (trucks, lorries, tempos) are NOT blocked. Only passenger vehicles (< 13 seats) are blocked.
- **Check the business use** — If the vehicle is used for: (a) further supply / dealing in vehicles, (b) transport of passengers (taxi, cab operator), (c) driving school — ITC is fully available.
- **Document:** Vehicle RC (Registration Certificate) showing seating capacity + business purpose.

---

### Ground 2 — ITC on Food / Catering Disallowed

**Officer's position:** ITC on canteen / outdoor catering / food — blocked.

**Defence:**
- **Statutory canteen obligation** — Under the Factories Act, companies with 250+ workers must maintain a canteen. ITC on canteen services mandated by law is NOT blocked (Circular 172/04/2022-GST).
- **Business purpose** — If food is provided at a business conference / client meeting as part of business activity, argue it is an input service for business, not "outdoor catering" in the personal benefit sense.
- **Cite Circular 172/04/2022:** Canteen services provided by employer to employees where mandatory under any law — ITC is available.

---

### Ground 3 — ITC on Construction / Works Contract Disallowed

**Officer's position:** ITC on works contract for building — blocked under §17(5)(c)/(d).

**Defence:**
- **Plant and machinery exception** — If the construction is for a manufacturing unit, factory building used as plant / machinery infrastructure — argue the structure qualifies as plant and machinery.
- **Further supply of works contract** — If the taxpayer is a works contractor themselves (construction company), ITC on sub-contract works contract is NOT blocked.
- **Repair and maintenance ≠ construction** — ITC on repair/maintenance of existing building is NOT blocked (only original construction is blocked). Cite the distinction.

---

### Ground 4 — ITC on Health Insurance / Life Insurance Disallowed

**Officer's position:** Insurance premium — blocked.

**Defence:**
- **Mandatory under law** — If health insurance is provided as mandated by any Central or State government notification, ITC is not blocked.
- **Group health insurance under employee obligation** — Check if a government notification mandates the specific insurance for the category of employees.
- Cite **Circular 172/04/2022-GST** which clarifies employer-provided health insurance and canteen.

---

### Ground 5 — ITC on Employee Benefits Disallowed

**Officer's position:** ITC on employee perquisites (cab, food, mobile) — blocked as "personal consumption."

**Defence:**
- **Mandatory vs voluntary** — If the service is mandatory for business operations (security, transportation for factory workers at odd hours) — argue it is a business input, not personal benefit.
- **Contractual obligation** — If the service is provided as per employment contract / CTC structure, it is a business cost, not a perquisite.
- **Direct nexus to business** — Cab service to bring employees to factory at 2 AM shift = business necessity, not personal benefit.

---

### Ground 6 — ITC Reversed on Goods Destroyed / Written Off

**Officer's position:** ITC must be reversed on goods lost / destroyed / written off (§17(5)(h)).

**Defence:**
- **Insurance claim received** — If insurance claim was received for the destroyed goods, the ITC reversal demand may be reduced proportionally.
- **Natural calamity / force majeure** — No exception in the Act, but argue on equitable grounds + file for relief.
- **Written off in books ≠ destroyed** — Goods written off in books for accounting purposes (slow moving, obsolete) may still physically exist. If goods are still in stock, reversal under §17(5)(h) does not apply.

## ITC Reversal Calculation (If Accepting)

```
ITC to reverse = ITC originally availed on the specific goods/services

Interest = Reversed ITC × 18% × Days ÷ 365
(Days = from date of availment to date of reversal)
```

Payment via DRC-03. Cite ARN in reply.

## Checklist — Before Accepting §17(5) Demand

- [ ] Does the item actually fall within the blocked list?
- [ ] Does any exception apply? (business use, mandatory by law, plant & machinery)
- [ ] Is the officer applying the correct sub-section of §17(5)?
- [ ] Has the correct CBIC circular been considered?
- [ ] Is the interest calculation correct (18%, net cash basis)?
- [ ] Is penalty proportionate (§73 — 10% max for non-fraud)?

## Key Circulars

- **Circular 172/04/2022-GST** — ITC on canteen, health insurance mandated by law
- **Circular 92/11/2019-GST** — Vehicles — ITC eligibility clarification
- **Circular 106/25/2019-GST** — Works contract and construction ITC

## Key Case Law

- *Safari Industries (India) Ltd v. UOI* (SC, 2024) — Plant and machinery definition; ITC on factory building may be available if it qualifies as plant
- *Inox Air Products* — Distinction between construction of building vs plant and machinery
- *Bharat Oman Refineries* — Works contract ITC for industrial plant
