# RCM Non-Payment — Defence Playbook

## What Is RCM?

Under the **Reverse Charge Mechanism (RCM)**, the liability to pay GST shifts from the supplier to the **recipient** of goods or services. The recipient pays tax directly to the government instead of paying it to the supplier.

- Governed by: **Section 9(3)** and **Section 9(4)** of CGST Act, 2017
- ITC on RCM paid is available under **Section 16** (subject to conditions)

---

## Common RCM SCN Scenarios

| SCN Allegation | What the officer is saying |
|---|---|
| RCM not paid on legal / advocate services | Advocate fees paid but GST under RCM not deposited |
| RCM not paid on GTA (Goods Transport Agency) | Freight paid to GTA but RCM GST not paid |
| RCM not paid on import of services | Foreign vendor payments — IGST under RCM not paid |
| RCM not paid on director's remuneration | Sitting fees / salary to director, RCM applicable |
| RCM not paid on security services | Labour supplier not registered — RCM applies |
| RCM under §9(4) — unregistered supplier | Purchases from unregistered dealers (limited applicability) |

---

## Key RCM Categories (Section 9(3) — Notified Services)

| Service | RCM Applicability |
|---|---|
| Legal services by advocate / firm to business | Yes — recipient pays RCM |
| Goods Transport Agency (GTA) — road freight | Yes — if GTA opts for 5% (no ITC) scheme |
| Sponsorship services | Yes — when provided to body corporate |
| Services by government / local authority | Specific services — check notification |
| Import of services (from foreign vendors) | Yes — IGST under RCM |
| Security services (supply of personnel) | Yes — if supplier is not registered |
| Director services to company | Yes — remuneration / sitting fees |
| Renting of motor vehicles | Specific conditions — check notification |

> Always verify against **Notification No. 13/2017-CT(Rate)** and amendments for the specific period.

## Defence Strategy

### Defence 1 — Service Does Not Fall Under RCM Notification

The officer must show that the specific service received falls under the RCM notification for that period. If the service is not notified → RCM does not apply.

**Steps:**
1. Identify the exact nature of the service received
2. Check Notification 13/2017-CT(Rate) for that FY
3. If not listed → RCM not applicable, demand fails
4. Cite the specific entry number of the notification

---

### Defence 2 — GTA — Supplier Opted for 12% (With ITC) Rate

If the GTA supplier had opted for the **12% GST rate (with ITC)**, RCM does not apply — the supplier charges GST directly.

**Documents:** GTA's invoice showing 12% GST charged + supplier's GST registration showing the 12% option.

---

### Defence 3 — Already Paid — RCM Discharged

If RCM was paid (even if in a different return period), produce:
- GSTR-3B showing RCM payment in cash ledger
- DRC-03 ARN if paid voluntarily after default
- Challan / payment reference for the period

**Note:** RCM must be paid in **cash** — cannot be set off from ITC credit ledger.

---

### Defence 4 — Amount Below Threshold (§9(4) Cases)

Section 9(4) RCM on purchases from unregistered dealers was suspended from 13-Oct-2017 and re-introduced with modifications. Verify whether the specific transaction and period falls within the operational window.

---

### Defence 5 — ITC Available on RCM Paid — Net Tax Effect Is Nil

Even if RCM was not paid in time, the taxpayer is entitled to ITC of the same amount in the same return period under §16. Therefore:

- RCM payable: ₹X
- ITC eligible on RCM paid: ₹X (same amount)
- **Net revenue impact on government: NIL**

This is a strong argument against imposition of penalty and interest — when ITC exactly offsets the RCM, there is no revenue loss.

*Cite: Multiple HC and AAR decisions holding that where ITC is available on RCM, the net demand is nil and penalty is not warranted.*

---

### Defence 6 — Interest Calculation Dispute

Interest under §50 on RCM runs only from the date the return was due to the date of actual payment. Verify:
- Correct due date (20th of following month for GSTR-3B)
- Correct number of days
- Interest applies only on the cash portion (Finance Act 2021)

---

## RCM Payment Procedure (For Accepting and Paying)

1. Pay RCM amount via **cash ledger** (not ITC ledger) — create challan
2. Report in **GSTR-3B**: Table 3.1(d) — Inward supplies liable to RCM
3. Simultaneously claim ITC in **GSTR-3B**: Table 4(A)(3) — ITC on RCM
4. Quote DRC-03 ARN in the SCN reply if paying voluntarily

---

## Reply Strategy for RCM SCN (DRC-06)

**Step 1 — Check if RCM actually applies**
- Is the service notified under §9(3)?
- Is the supplier registered or unregistered?
- What period is involved?

**Step 2 — If RCM does not apply:** Contest entirely, cite the notification entry that does not cover the service.

**Step 3 — If RCM applies but was paid late:** Pay via DRC-03, cite payment ARN, argue nil penalty since ITC offsets.

**Step 4 — If RCM applies and was never paid:** Pay now via DRC-03 + interest. Claim ITC in current GSTR-3B simultaneously. Argue reduced penalty since net government loss is nil.

## Key Notifications

- **Notification 13/2017-CT(Rate)** — Services under RCM (§9(3)) — amended multiple times
- **Notification 07/2019-CT(Rate)** — Security services RCM added
- **Notification 05/2017-IT(Rate)** — Import of services RCM

---

## Key Case Law

- *Sutherland Global Services v. AACST* — RCM + simultaneous ITC = nil revenue impact, penalty not warranted
- *Safari Industries (India) Ltd* — GTA RCM applicability depends on rate option chosen by supplier
- Multiple AAR rulings on director remuneration RCM applicability
