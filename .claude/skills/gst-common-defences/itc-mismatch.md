# ITC Mismatch Defence — GSTR-2A/2B vs GSTR-3B

## The Allegation
Officer claims: "ITC of ₹X claimed in GSTR-3B but invoices not reflecting in GSTR-2A/2B — wrongly availed."

## 5-Point Defence

**1. GSTR-2A is not the sole criterion**
Section 16(2) lists 5 conditions for ITC — GSTR-2A/2B match is NOT one of them for periods before 01.01.2022. Condition (aa) inserted only w.e.f. 01.01.2022.

**2. Supplier's default ≠ recipient's fault**
If supplier filed GSTR-1 late or not at all, recipient cannot be penalised for supplier's default. Cite: *D.Y. Beathel Enterprises v. STO (Madras HC, 2021)*, *Hindustan Herbal Cosmetics v. UP (Allahabad HC, 2022)*.

**3. Produce independent evidence of genuineness**
Submit: tax invoice, e-way bill, goods receipt note (GRN), lorry receipt, bank payment proof showing GST paid to supplier, supplier GSTIN active at time of transaction.

**4. Supplier's GSTIN was active and tax deposited**
Check on GST portal: was supplier's GSTIN active? Has supplier's return been filed? If yes — tax was deposited by supplier, condition (c) of §16(2) is satisfied.

**5. Rule 36(4) cap was complied with**
For periods Oct 2019 – Dec 2021: ITC capped at 110%/105% of GSTR-2B. Show that total ITC claimed was within the permitted cap even if individual invoices are mismatched.

## Documents to Attach to Reply
- Reconciliation statement: GSTR-2B vs books vs GSTR-3B
- Copies of tax invoices
- E-way bills
- GRNs / delivery challans
- Bank statements showing payment to supplier with GST
- Screenshot of supplier GSTIN status on GST portal
