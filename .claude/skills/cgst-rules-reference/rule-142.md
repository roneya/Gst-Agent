# Rule 142 — Notice and Order for Demand of Amounts Payable Under the Act

## Plain-English Summary

Rule 142 is the **procedural engine** for GST demand proceedings. Every step in a §73/§74/§74A demand — from the officer's pre-SCN intimation to the taxpayer's voluntary payment to the final demand order — has a prescribed DRC (Demand and Recovery) form.

Knowing which form does what is essential for:
- Correctly filing voluntary payments
- Triggering the no-penalty windows under §73(8)/§74(8)/§74A(8)/(9)
- Ensuring the officer issues the right closure form
- Challenging defective notices (wrong form = invalid notice)

## Rule 142(1) — SCN Summary and Statement Summary

### DRC-01 — Summary of SCN
When the officer issues an SCN under §73/§74/§74A (or other sections like §52, §76, §122–§130), he must **simultaneously** upload a summary in **FORM GST DRC-01** electronically.

**What DRC-01 contains**:
- GSTIN and taxpayer name
- Tax period(s) covered
- Section under which notice is issued
- Amount of tax, interest, and penalty as quantified

**Agent alert**: DRC-01 is not the SCN itself — it is the **summary** of the SCN. The SCN (in DRC-01 format on the portal) is the formal demand document. If the officer serves a letter but does not upload DRC-01 on the portal, the notice may not be a valid GST notice for limitation purposes.

### DRC-02 — Summary of Statement (Additional Periods)
When the officer issues a §73(3)/§74(3)/§74A(3) statement covering additional periods beyond the original SCN, the statement summary is uploaded in **FORM GST DRC-02**.

## Rule 142(1A) — Pre-SCN Intimation via DRC-01A Part A

**Before issuing the SCN**, the proper officer **may** (discretionary) communicate the proposed tax, interest, and penalty to the taxpayer in **Part A of FORM GST DRC-01A**.

**Purpose**: Give the taxpayer a chance to pay or respond before a formal SCN is issued — saving both sides from the SCN process.

**Key word: "may"** — This is not mandatory. The officer can proceed directly to SCN without issuing DRC-01A. However, if the officer does issue DRC-01A, it opens up the pre-SCN voluntary payment route.

**Agent alert**: If the taxpayer receives a DRC-01A Part A, this is the **golden opportunity** to resolve the matter before an SCN — pay under §73(5)/§74(5)/§74A(8)(i)/§74A(9)(i) and avoid SCN + penalty entirely.

## Rule 142(2) — Voluntary Payment via DRC-03

When the taxpayer makes a **voluntary / pre-SCN payment** (under §73(5), §74(5), §74A(8)(i), §74A(9)(i), or any other self-payment), the taxpayer must:

1. File **FORM GST DRC-03** on the portal
2. Specify: tax amount, interest, penalty (if applicable), tax period, and reason/cause
3. The portal auto-generates **FORM GST DRC-04** as acknowledgement

### DRC-03 "Cause" options — which to select

| Scenario | Cause to select in DRC-03 |
|---|---|
| Voluntary pre-SCN payment (§73(5)/§74(5)/§74A(8)(i)/§74A(9)(i)) | **"Voluntary"** |
| Payment in response to ASMT-10 / §61 scrutiny discrepancy | **"Scrutiny"** |
| Payment after SCN within 30/60-day window (§73(8)/§74(8)/§74A(8)(ii)/§74A(9)(ii)) | **"SCN"** → select SCN reference |
| Payment in response to audit finding (§65/§66) | **"Audit"** |
| Payment of demand as ascertained by officer (DRC-01A) | **"DRC-01A"** |

### After filing DRC-03
- Write to the officer quoting the DRC-03 ARN and citing the relevant sub-section (§73(5) / §73(8) / §74A(8)(ii) etc.)
- In the reply (DRC-06 or ASMT-11), quote the DRC-03 ARN and state that proceedings stand concluded

## Rule 142(2A) — Taxpayer Response to DRC-01A (Part B)

If the taxpayer received a **DRC-01A Part A** (pre-SCN intimation) and:
- Has made **partial payment**, OR
- Wants to **file submissions** against the proposed liability

The taxpayer files **Part B of FORM GST DRC-01A** on the portal.

The officer may then issue **Part C of FORM GST DRC-01A** accepting the payment or submissions.

**Agent tip**: Use Part B to put your defence on record even at the pre-SCN stage. If the officer accepts Part B, no SCN is issued. If not, the Part B submissions can be repeated in the DRC-06 reply to the eventual SCN.

---

## Rule 142(2B) — Linking a DRC-03 to a Specific Demand Entry (DRC-03A)

**Problem this solves**: Sometimes a taxpayer files DRC-03 as a voluntary payment, but it gets credited to the electronic liability register without being linked to the specific demand/SCN. This creates a mismatch — the demand shows as outstanding even though money has been paid.

**Solution**: File **FORM GST DRC-03A** to link the DRC-03 payment to the specific debit entry in the Electronic Liability Register (FORM GST PMT-01) for that demand.

**Proviso**: DRC-03A cannot be filed if DRC-05 (closure order) has already been issued for that payment — the matter is already closed.

**Agent alert**: If a client paid via DRC-03 but the demand still shows as outstanding on the portal, advise filing DRC-03A to reconcile the payment against the demand.

## Rule 142(3) — Post-SCN Payment and DRC-05 Closure

When the taxpayer makes payment **within the no-penalty window after SCN**:
- **§73(8)**: tax + interest within 30 days of SCN
- **§74(8)**: tax + interest + 25% penalty within 30 days of SCN
- **§74A(8)(ii)**: tax + interest within 60 days of SCN (non-fraud)
- **§74A(9)(ii)**: tax + interest + 25% penalty within 60 days of SCN (fraud)

**Process**:
1. Taxpayer files **DRC-03** on portal (cause: "SCN", select SCN reference)
2. Taxpayer informs the officer with DRC-03 ARN
3. Officer issues **FORM GST DRC-05** — **concluding the proceedings**

**DRC-05 = Proceedings closed.** No further action by the officer.

**Agent alert**: Always follow up the DRC-03 filing with a written communication to the officer requesting issuance of DRC-05. If the officer delays DRC-05, it does not mean the proceedings are still open — the statutory deemed conclusion under §73(8)/§74A(8)(ii) operates automatically upon payment within the deadline.

## Rule 142(4) — Reply to SCN via DRC-06

The taxpayer's **reply / representation** to the SCN (under §73(9)/§74(9)/§74A(6)) must be filed in **FORM GST DRC-06**.

DRC-06 is the formal reply form on the GST portal — the agent must ensure the reply is uploaded here in addition to any physical/email submission.

**What DRC-06 contains**:
- Reference to DRC-01 (SCN summary)
- Taxpayer's submissions and explanations
- Documents uploaded as annexures
- Prayer for dropping / reducing the demand

**Agent alert**: DRC-06 on the portal has a character limit for text. For detailed replies, upload the full reply as a PDF attachment in DRC-06 and include a brief summary in the text field.

---

## Rule 142(5) — Demand Order Summary in DRC-07

After passing the order (under §73(9)/§74(9)/§74A(6)), the officer uploads a summary in **FORM GST DRC-07**, specifying:
- Tax amount confirmed
- Interest payable
- Penalty imposed

**DRC-07 = the demand order summary on the portal.** This is the document used for:
- Appeal filing (the appellant references DRC-07)
- Recovery proceedings
- DRC-03A linkage (for post-order payments)

**Rule 142(6)**: The DRC-07 order is treated as a **Notice for Recovery** — the department can initiate recovery under §79 based on DRC-07 without any further notice.

---

## Rule 142(7) — Rectification / Withdrawal via DRC-08

If the officer rectifies the order under §161 (rectification of errors apparent from the record) or withdraws the order, the summary of the rectification/withdrawal is uploaded in **FORM GST DRC-08**.

**Agent alert**: If a demand order has an arithmetical error, wrong tax period, or clerical mistake — apply to the officer under §161 for rectification. The corrected DRC-07 / DRC-08 will reflect the corrected amounts.

## The Full DRC Lifecycle — Demand Proceeding Flowchart

```
Officer detects discrepancy
          ↓
Optional: DRC-01A Part A (pre-SCN intimation)
          ↓
  ┌───────────────────┐
  │ Taxpayer responds │
  │ DRC-01A Part B    │ → Officer issues DRC-01A Part C (accepts) → Matter closed
  │ OR pays DRC-03    │
  └───────────────────┘
          ↓ (if not resolved)
Officer issues SCN + DRC-01 (summary)
          ↓
  ┌────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │ Taxpayer pays within 30/60 days        │
  │ (§73(8)/§74(8)/§74A(8)(ii)/§74A(9)(ii))│
  │ Files DRC-03                           │ → Officer issues DRC-05 (closure)
  └────────────────────────────────────────┘
          ↓ (if not paid within window)
Taxpayer files DRC-06 (reply/representation)
          ↓
Officer considers reply
          ↓
Officer issues order + uploads DRC-07
          ↓
  ┌──────────────────────────────────┐
  │ Post-order fraud payment (§74/   │
  │ §74A(9)(iii)) within 30/60 days  │
  │ Files DRC-03                     │ → All proceedings concluded
  └──────────────────────────────────┘
          ↓ (if not paid)
DRC-07 becomes recovery notice (Rule 142(6))
Recovery proceedings under §79
```

## Procedural Defects — DRC Form Errors

| # | Defect | Consequence / Argument |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | SCN served without DRC-01 uploaded on portal | Notice not properly served under Rule 142(1) |
| 2 | DRC-01 uploaded with wrong section reference | Notice may be invalid if wrong section changes the limitation / penalty |
| 3 | DRC-03 filed but DRC-05 not issued by officer despite timely payment | File representation citing §73(8)/§74A(8)(ii) — proceedings are deemed concluded by statute even without DRC-05 |
| 4 | DRC-07 order not uploaded after personal order passed | Recovery cannot commence until DRC-07 is uploaded (Rule 142(5)/(6)) |
| 5 | DRC-03 payment not linked to demand — demand shows outstanding | File DRC-03A to link payment to the demand debit entry |
| 6 | DRC-08 not issued after rectification order | The original DRC-07 continues to show wrong figures — follow up for DRC-08 |

## Key Tips for the Agent

1. **Always check DRC-01 exists** before treating a notice as a valid §73/§74/§74A SCN. No DRC-01 = procedurally defective notice.

2. **DRC-03 ARN is the proof of payment** — quote it in every reply, every closure request, every appeal.

3. **DRC-05 is the officer's acknowledgement** of closure — but the legal closure happens the moment the taxpayer pays within the statutory window. DRC-05 is declaratory, not constitutive.

4. **DRC-07 triggers recovery** — once uploaded, the department can recover without further notice. If a client disputes the order, file the appeal quickly. Filing an appeal stays recovery in most cases.

5. **DRC-03A fixes payment linking problems** — use it whenever a voluntary DRC-03 payment is not reflected against the specific demand.
