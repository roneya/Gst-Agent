# Rule 36(4) — Restriction on ITC Not Reflected in GSTR-2B

## What Is Rule 36(4)?

Rule 36(4) restricts a registered person from claiming ITC beyond the amount reflected in their **GSTR-2B** (auto-drafted ITC statement) for that month/quarter.

This is one of the most common grounds for GST SCNs — officers demand reversal of ITC that the taxpayer claimed but which was not appearing in GSTR-2B for that period.

---

## Evolution of the Restriction

| Period | Rule 36(4) Limit |
|---|---|
| Oct 2019 – Dec 2020 | ITC restricted to 110% of GSTR-2A eligible credit |
| Jan 2021 – Aug 2021 | ITC restricted to 105% of GSTR-2A eligible credit |
| Sep 2021 onwards | ITC restricted to **100% of GSTR-2B** eligible credit |

> **Current position (Sep 2021 onwards):** No ITC can be claimed beyond what appears in GSTR-2B for that tax period.

---

## How GSTR-2B Works

- GSTR-2B is a **static, auto-populated** statement generated on the 14th of each month
- It reflects invoices uploaded by the supplier in their GSTR-1 / IFF up to the cut-off date
- Late GSTR-1 filed by supplier → invoice appears in next month's GSTR-2B
- GSTR-2B for a month **cannot be amended** after generation

---

## Common SCN Ground — "ITC Availed in Excess of GSTR-2B"

The officer compares:
- ITC claimed in GSTR-3B for the period
- ITC appearing in GSTR-2B for the same period

If GSTR-3B ITC > GSTR-2B ITC → SCN for excess ITC reversal + interest + penalty

---

## Defence Strategy

### Defence 1 — Supplier Filed GSTR-1 Late (ITC in Subsequent GSTR-2B)

**Position:** The ITC claimed in GSTR-3B for Month X appeared in GSTR-2B for Month X+1 (or later) because the supplier filed GSTR-1 late. The ITC is genuinely eligible and has since been reflected in GSTR-2B.

**Documents:** Supplier's GSTR-1 filing date + GSTR-2B of subsequent month showing the invoice.

**Legal ground:** Rule 36(4) restricts availment in the current period but does not deny ITC permanently. Where the supplier has paid tax and the invoice appears in a subsequent GSTR-2B, the ITC is eligible in that subsequent period. The difference is a timing issue, not an ineligibility issue.

---

### Defence 2 — Reconciliation: GSTR-2B vs Books

Prepare a detailed reconciliation showing:
- Total ITC as per books of account
- ITC as per GSTR-2B
- Difference and reason for each line item (late supplier filing, credit notes, etc.)

**Documents:** Purchase register, GSTR-2B for all relevant months, supplier-wise reconciliation.

---

### Defence 3 — Supplier Has Paid Tax (§16(2) Conditions Met)

Even where GSTR-2B mismatch exists, if the taxpayer can show:
1. Invoice received and goods/services received
2. Tax paid to supplier (bank transfer proof)
3. Supplier has filed GSTR-3B and paid tax to government

→ The fundamental condition of §16(2) is met. GSTR-2B mismatch alone cannot deny ITC when underlying conditions are satisfied.

*Key case law: Dyna Automation Pvt Ltd (Guj HC), Suncraft Energy Pvt Ltd (Cal HC) — GSTR-2A/2B is a facilitating tool, not a bar to ITC eligibility*

---

### Defence 4 — Finance Act 2022 Amendment to §16(2)(aa)

Section 16(2)(aa) (inserted by Finance Act 2022, w.e.f. 01-Oct-2022) made GSTR-2B matching a **statutory condition** for ITC from Oct 2022 onwards. For periods **before Oct 2022**, GSTR-2B matching was only a rule-level restriction, not a statutory bar — and rules cannot override the Act.

**Period-wise defence:**

| Period | Legal position |
|---|---|
| Before 01-Oct-2022 | Rule 36(4) restriction only — not statutory bar; §16(2) conditions override |
| From 01-Oct-2022 | §16(2)(aa) is statutory — GSTR-2B matching is mandatory |

---

### Defence 5 — GSTR-2B Error / System Glitch

If the invoice was uploaded by supplier in GSTR-1 correctly but did not appear in GSTR-2B due to system error → raise with GST helpdesk + present supplier's GSTR-1 filing confirmation as evidence.

---

## Calculation — How Much ITC Is Disallowable?

```
Excess ITC = ITC claimed in GSTR-3B − ITC in GSTR-2B

If supplier filed late and invoice now appears in subsequent GSTR-2B:
Net disallowable = Excess ITC − ITC reversed in subsequent month's GSTR-3B
```

---

## What to Do When ITC Mismatch Exists

1. Run month-wise GSTR-2B vs GSTR-3B reconciliation for the disputed period
2. Identify invoices causing the mismatch (supplier-wise)
3. Contact suppliers to verify if GSTR-1 was filed and tax paid
4. If supplier confirms filing → obtain GSTR-1 acknowledgement and GSTR-2B excerpt from subsequent month
5. Prepare reconciliation statement for DRC-06 reply
6. For genuinely ineligible ITC → reverse in current GSTR-3B and pay interest via DRC-03

---

## Key Circulars and Notifications

- **Circular 123/42/2019-GST** — Clarification on GSTR-2A and ITC availment
- **Notification 39/2021-CT** — Amendment to Rule 36(4) limiting ITC to 100% of GSTR-2B
- **Finance Act 2022** — §16(2)(aa) inserted making GSTR-2B matching statutory from 01-Oct-2022

---

## Key Case Law

- *Dyna Automation Pvt Ltd v. Union of India* (Guj HC) — GSTR-2A mismatch alone not sufficient to deny ITC
- *Suncraft Energy Pvt Ltd v. ASGST* (Cal HC) — ITC cannot be denied solely on GSTR-2B mismatch without examining underlying documents
- *Philco Appliances Ltd* — taxpayer cannot be penalised for supplier's default in filing
